 1.    FLOW TITLE:  CASE 8671; RAINBIRD, W.J.,
       POINTED AXISYMMETRIC BODIES AT ANGLE OF ATTACK. (SUPERSONIC)

 2.    REVISION DATE: NOVEMBER 6, 1981.

 3.    EVALUATORS: PEAKE, D., MOFFETT FIELD CALIFORNIA, 94035;
       COCKRELL, D.J., UNIVERSITY OF LEICESTER, ENGINEERING DEPARTMENT,
       ENGLAND.

 4.    EXPERIMENT LOCATION AND DATE: 1.5 M INTERMITTENT BLOWDOWN
       WIND TUNNEL, NATIONAL AERONAUTICAL ESTABLISHMENT (NAE),
       OTTAWA, CANADA, 1968

 5.    ABSTRACT OF EXPERIMENT:
            THE FLOW FIELD TO BE CALCULATED CONSISTS OF A 12.5 DEG.
       SEMI-ANGLE SHARPLY-POINTED CONE SET AT AN ANGLE OF INCIDENCE
       OF 2.75 DEG. IN A SUPERSONIC FLOW AT M = 1.8 AND REYNOLDS
       NUMBER BASED ON CONE LENGTH (L) OF 2.5 * 10**7. IN THE EXPERIMENT,
       THE STAGNATION PRESSURE AND TEMPERATURE WERE 1.8 ATMOSPHERES
       AND 21 DEG. C RESPECTIVELY.
            UNDER THESE CONDITIONS, THE FLOW FIELD IS NOT WHOLLY
       CONICAL. HOWEVER, IN SOME FLOW EVALUATION METHODS IT MAY BE
       NECESSARY TO ASSUME CONICALITY WITH LITTLE OVERALL LOSS IN
       ACCURACY. ASSUME THAT THE BOUNDARY LAYER IS FULLY TURBULENT
       DOWNSTREAM OF THE BOW SHOCK.

 6.    REFERENCES:
       1)   RAINBIRD, W. J., "TURBULENT BOUNDARY LAYER GROWTH AND
       SEPARATION ON A YAWED CONE." AIAA JOURNAL, VOL. 6, P.2410, 1968
       2)   RAINBIRD, W. J., "THE EXTERNAL FLOW FIELD ABOUT YAWED
       CIRCULAR CONES." AGARD CP.30, P. 19.1, 1968
       3)   PEAKE, D. J., AND TOBAK, M., "THREE-DIMENSIONAL INTERACTIONS
       AND VORTICAL FLOWS WITH EMPHASIS ON HIGH SPEEDS."
       NASA TM 81169, 1980 AND AGARDOGRAPH 252, JULY 1980
       4)   MCRAE, D. S., PEAKE, D. J., AND FISHER, D. F., "A

       13TH FLUID AND PLASMA DYNAMICS CONFERENCE, AIAA-80-1422, 1980

 7.    INSTRUMENTATION:
            CIRCUMFERENTIAL PRESSURE DISTRIBUTIONS WERE MEASURED
       USING UNBONDED STRAIN-GAGE PRESSURE TRANSDUCERS CLOSELY COUPLED
       PNEUMATICALLY TO 0.0005 M DIA. HOLES SPACED 45 DEG. APART AT
       THE MEASUREMENT STATION - X/L = 0.85 AFT OF THE APEX OF THE CONE.
       MAGNITUDE AND DIRECTION OF SHEAR STRESSES WERE DETERMINED
       BY USING A THREE-TUBE  MEASURING HEAD BOUNDARY LAYER PROBE
       TOUCHING THE CONE SURFACE (SEE REF. 1). MAGNITUDE WAS ALSO
       MEASURED BY THE USE OF SMALL PRESTON TUBES (O.D. = 0.000419 M AND
       I.D. = 0.000191 M, (SEE REF. 2)) AND LOCAL MACH NUMBERS,
       PITOT PRESSURES AND FLOW DIRECTION IN THE EXTERNAL FLOW
       ABOVE THE CONE WERE ALL MEASURED USING FLOW FIELD PROBES (REF. 2).
       OVERALL FORCE MEASUREMENTS WERE MADE WITH AN INTERNAL STRAIN-
       GAGE BALANCE.

 8.    EXPERIMENTAL PARAMETERS:

       REINF - REYNOLDS NUMBER BASED ON CONE LENGTH AND UNDISTURBED
               CONDITIONS = 24 * 10**6 TO 34 * 10**6

 11.   TAPE ORGANIZATION:
            THE TAPE IS A 2400 FOOT, ODD PARITY, PHASE ENCODED
       UNLABELLED TAPE WRITTEN AT A DENSITY OF 1600 BITS PER INCH ACCORDING
       TO EBCDIC CODE. THE RECORD FORMAT IS FIXED AND BLOCKED; RECORD
       LENGTH = 80 BYTES; 100 RECORD PER BLOCK; BLOCKSIZE = 8000 BYTES.
            NORMALIZED DATA ARE CREATED FROM MEASURED DATA AS FOLLOWS:
       XNORM = (X - XMIN)/(XMAX - XMIN)
       NORMALIZED VALUES ARE INTEGERIZED BY MULTIPLYING BY 10000 AND
       ROUNDING UP OR DOWN TO THE NEAREST INTEGER.
       IXNORM = XNORM * 10000.
       THUS EACH NORMALIZED DATUM IS WRITTEN ON TO THE TAPE AS AN INTEGER
       VALUE BETWEEN 0 AND 10000, HOWEVER THE MAXIMAS AND THE MINIMAS ARE
       WRITTEN ON TO THE TAPE AS NORMALIZED VALUES.
       ALL NULL DATA ARE WRITTEN AS 20000.
       THE EQUATION DESCRIBING THE RELATION BETWEEN ACTUAL DATA AND
       THE NORMALIZED DATA ON THE TAPE IS:
       X = XMIN + (((XMAX - XMIN) * IXNORM)/10000.)
       WHERE X, XMAX AND XMIN ARE REAL AND IXNORM IS AN INTEGER.
