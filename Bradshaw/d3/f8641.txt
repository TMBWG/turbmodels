1.  FLOW TITLE:  CASE 8641;  SETTLES, G.S., BACA, B.K., WILLIAMS, D.R.
    AND BOGDONOFF, S.M.; "REATTACHING PLANAR FREE SHEAR LAYER(SUPERSONIC)"

2.  REVISION DATE:  NOVEMBER 7, 1981.

3.  EVALUATORS: RUBESIN, M. W., AND HORSTMAN, C. C.,
    NASA AMES RESEARCH CENTER, MOFFETT FIELD, CA. 94035.

4.  DATE AND LOCATION OF EXPERIMENT:  1979-1980, GAS-DYNAMICS
    LABORATORY, FORRESTAL CAMPUS, PRINCETON UNIVERSITY, PRINCETON,
    NJ 08544.

5.  ABSTRACT:  IN THIS EXPERIMENTAL STUDY, A TWO-DIMENSIONAL FREE SHEAR
    LAYER AT M = 2.92 AND RE = 6.7E 07/M  REATTACHES ON AN
    INCLINED SURFACE.  THE EXPERIMENTAL GEOMETRY CONSISTED OF A FLAT
    PLATE WITH A RECTANGULAR CAVITY TERMINATED BY A 20 DEGREE REATTACH-
    MENT RAMP.  THIS GEOMETRY WAS SPECIFICALLY DESIGNED TO CREATE AN
    UNDISTURBED, CONSTANT-PRESSURE FREE SHEAR LAYER WHICH FORMS A WELL-
    DEFINED INITIAL CONDITION FOR THE REATTACHMENT PROCESS.  THE
    EXPERIMENT WAS CARRIED OUT IN THE PRINCETON UNIVERSITY 0.2 X 0.2
    M, HIGH REYNOLDS NUMBER SUPERSONIC BLOW-DOWN WIND TUNNEL.  SURFACE
    AND FLOWFIELD MEASUREMENTS ARE GIVEN AT SELECTED STATIONS THROUGH-
    OUT THE FLOW.

7.  INSTRUMENTATION:
    THE WALL STATIC PRESSURE DISTRIBUTION WAS SENSED THROUGH ORIFICES
    INSTALLED IN THE PLATE, CAVITY, AND RAMP.  SKIN FRICTION WAS
    ESTIMATED BY LEAST-SQUARES CURVE FITS OF THE COMPRESSIBLE WALL-WAKE
    LAW TO THE MEASURED VELOCITY PROFILES, AND FROM PRESTON TUBE
    MEASUREMENTS.  THE FLOW REATTACHMENT LOCATION WAS FOUND FROM SURFACE
    STREAK METHODS, AND WAS CONFIRMED BY THE OTHER MEASUREMENTS.  MEAN
    FLOW PROFILES WERE OBTAINED FROM SURVEYS OF PITOT PRESSURE AND
    STATIC PRESSURE, USING THE CAREFULLY JUSTIFIED ASSUMPTION OF CONST-
    ANT TOTAL TEMPERATURE.

11. TAPE ORGANIZATION:

    TO EBCDIC CODE.  THE RECORD FORMAT IS FIXED AND BLOCKED; RECORD
    LENGTH = 80 BYTES; 100 RECORDS PER BLOCK; BLOCKSIZE = 8000 BYTES.

    NORMALIZED DATA ARE CREATED FROM MEASURED DATA AS FOLLOWS:
    XNORM = (X - XMIN)/(XMAX - XMIN)
    THUS EACH NORMALIZED DATUM IS WRITTEN ONTO TAPE AS A VALUE
    BETWEEN 0.0 AND 1.0.
    ALL NULL DATA ARE WRITTEN AS 2.0.
    THE EQUATION DESCRIBING THE RELATION BETWEEN ACTUAL DATA AND
    THE NORMALIZED DATA ON THE TAPE IS
    X = XMIN + ((XMAX - XMIN) * XNORM)
    WHERE X, XMAX, XMIN AND XNORM ARE REAL.
