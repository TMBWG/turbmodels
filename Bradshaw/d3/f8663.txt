1.   FLOW TITLE:   CASE 8663;   KUSSOY, M. I., VIEGAS, J. R., AND               
HORSTMAN, C. C.; "INVESTIGATION OF 3-D SHOCK SEPARATED TURBULENT                
BOUNDARY LAYER."                                                                
                                                                                
2.   REVISION DATE:   NOVEMBER 8, 1981.                                         
                                                                                
3.   EVALUATORS:   RUBESIN, M. W., AND HORSTMAN, C. C., NASA AMES               
RESEARCH CENTER, MOFFETT FIELD, CA. 94035.                                      
                                                                                
4.   DATE AND LOCATION:   1979-1980,  HIGH REYNOLDS CHANNEL, NASA               
AMES RESEARCH CENTER, MOFFETT FIELD, CA. 94035.                                 
                                                                                
5.   ABSTRACT:   A DETAILED INVESTIGATION OF A FLOW IN WHICH A                  
THREE-DIMENSIONAL SHOCK WAVE SEPARATES A TWO-DIMENSIONAL TURBULENT              
BOUNDARY LAYER HAS BEEN MADE.  THE RESULTING FLOW FIELD IS HIGHLY               
THREE-DIMENSIONAL WITH A SIGNIFICANT PORTION OF FLOW SEPARATION ON              
THE SURFACE AT THE  PHI = 0  DEGREE (WINDWARD) PLANE AS WELL AS A               
LARGE ZONE OF SECONDARY SURFACE FLOW OFF THIS PLANE.  MEAN AND                  
FLUCTUATING EXPERIMENTAL MEASUREMENTS WERE OBTAINED THROUGHOUT THE              
ENTIRE FLOW FIELD.  THESE MEASUREMENTS INCLUDED MEAN PRESSURES,                
FLOW ANGLES AND SHEAR ON THE SURFACES, AS WELL AS YAW ANGLES, STATIC            
PRESSURES, TURBULENT SHEAR STRESSES AND TURBULENT KINETIC ENERGIES              
ON SELECTED PLANES THROUGHOUT THE FLOW FIELD.                                   
                                                                                
6.   REFERENCES:                                                                
                                                                                
     1.  KUSSOY, M. I., VIEGAS, J. R., AND HORSTMAN, C. C.;                     
     "AN EXPERIMENTAL AND NUMERICAL INVESTIGATION OF A 3-D SHOCK                
     SEPARATED TURBULENT BOUNDARY LAYER.  AIAA PAPER #80-0002,                  
     JAN. 1980.  ALSO TO BE PUBLISHED AS AN ARTICLE IN THE AIAA                 
     JOURNAL, DEC. 1980, TO MARCH 1981.                                         
                                                                                
7.   INSTRUMENTATION:   MEAN FLOW:  WALL PRESSURE TAPS AND BURIED               
WIRE GAGES WERE USED TO MEASURE PRESSURE AND SHEAR RESPECTIVELY                 
ON THE SURFACE.  FLOW FIELD PITOT AND STATIC PRESSURE MEASUREMENTS              
ON THE WINDWARD AND LEEWARD DATA PLANES WERE MADE USING SPECIALLY               
DESIGNED MINIATURE PROBES.  YAW ANGLE AND PITOT MEASUREMENTS                    
AT OTHER AZIMUTHAL DATA PLANES WERE MADE USING A COBRA PROBE. YAW               
ANGLE MEASUREMENTS WERE ALSO OBTAINED WITH A DUAL HOT-FILM GAGE.                
FLUCTUATING MEASUREMENTS:  BOTH SINGLE HOT-WIRE AND DUAL WEDGE                  
HOT-FILM PROBES WERE USED TO OBTAIN THE THREE FLUCTUATING VELOCITY              
COMPONENTS AND TURBULENT SHEAR STRESS IN THE WINDWARD AND LEEWARD               
PLANES.

    11. TAPE ORGANIZATION: THE TAPE IS A 2400 FOOT, 9 TRACK, ODD PARITY,        
    PHASE ENCODED, UNLABELLED TAPE WRITTEN AT A DENSITY OF 1600 BITS            
    PER INCH ACCORDING TO EBCDIC CODE.  THE RECORD FORMAT IS FIXED AND          
    BLOCKED; RECORD LENGTH = 80 BYTES; BLOCKSIZE = 8000 BYTES                   
                                                                                
    NORMALIZED DATA ARE CREATED FROM MEASURED DATA AS FOLLOWS:                  
    XNORM = (X - XMIN)/(XMAX - XMIN)                                            
    NORMALIZED VALUES ARE INTEGERIZED BY MULTIPLYING BY 10000 AND               
    ROUNDING UP OR DOWN TO THE NEAREST INTEGER.                                 
    IXNORM = XNORM * 10000.                                                     
    THUS EACH NORMALIZED DATUM IS WRITTEN ONTO TAPE AS AN INTEGER               
    VALUE BETWEEN 0 AND 10000.                                                  
    ALL NULL DATA ARE WRITTEN AS 20000                                          
    THE EQUATION DESCRIBING THE RELATION BETWEEN ACTUAL DATA AND                
    THE NORMALIZED DATA ON TAPE IS                                              
    X = XMIN + (((XMAX - XMIN) * IXNORM)/10000.)                                
    WHERE X, XMAX AND XMIN ARE REAL AND IXNORM IS INTEGER.
