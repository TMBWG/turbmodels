      1.     FLOW TITLE:  CASE 8411;  ZWARTS, F. J., "BOUNDARY LAYER
             IN ADVERSE PRESSURE GRADIENT".

      2.     REVISION DATE:  NOVEMBER 18, 1980

      3.     EVALUATORS:  RUBESIN, M. W. AND HORSTMAN, C. C., NASA
             AMES RESEARCH CENTER, MOFFETT FIELD CA 94035

      4.     EXPERIMENT LOCATION AND DATE:  NATIONAL RESEARCH COUNCIL,
             OTTAWA, CANADA   1975

      5.     ABSTRACT OF EXPERIMENT:  AN EXPERIMENT WAS CONDUCTED
             IN A TWO-DIMENSIONAL CHANNEL WITH A COMPRESSION WEDGE
             USED TO CREATE AN ADVERSE PRESSURE GRADIENT ON THE FLAT
             TEST WALL.  THE STUDY WAS PERFORMED AT A FREE-STREAM
             MACH NUMBER OF 4 IN THE NAE 5 INCH X 5 INCH BLOWDOWN
             WIND TUNNEL.  THE REYNOLDS NUMBER BASED ON THE UNDISTURBED
             MOMENTUM BOUNDARY LAYER THICKNESS WAS 3.5 X 10**4.

      6.     REFERENCES:  1. ZWARTS, F. J. "THE COMPRESSIBLE TURBULENT
             BOUNDARY LAYER IN A PRESSURE GRADIENT", PH.D. THESIS,
             MCGILL UNIVERSITY, MONTREAL, 1970.
                          2. FERNHOLZ, H. H. , FINLEY, P. J. " A
             CRITICAL COMPILATION OF COMPRESSIBLE TURBULENT BOUNDARY
             LAYER DATA", AGARDOGRAPH 223, 1977 (FLOW 7007).

      7.     INSTRUMENTATION
             STATIC PRESSURE ORIFICES AND THERMOCOUPLES WERE USED TO
             MEASURE WALL PRESSURE AND TEMPERATURE.  PRESTON PROBES
             WERE USED TO OBTAIN SURFACE SKIN FRICTION.  MEAN FLOW
             PROFILES WERE OBTAINED FROM PITOT PROBE SURVEYS ASSUMING
             STATIC PRESSURE AND TOTAL TEMPERATURES CONSTANT THROUGH
             THE BOUNDARY LAYER.

      8.     GLOBAL PARAMETERS
             INFINITY CONDITIONS ARE DEFINED AT X = -0.00635 M.
             XMINF = 4.0203
             PINF = 9.4452E 03  N/M**2
             UINF = 672.31 M/S
             TEMPERATURES AT X = -0.00635 M ARE
             TTOT = 21.84 DEGREES CENTIGRADE. (295 KELVIN)
             TWALL = 1.84 DEGREES CENTIGRADE. (275 KELVIN)
             TSTAT = -203.594 DEGREES CENTIGRADE. (69.566 KELVIN)
             BOUNDARY LAYER QUANTITIES AT X = -0.00635 M. ARE
             DEL = 0.01088 M.
             DELS = 0.00447 M.
             THETA = 0.00056 M.

      9.     MEASURED VARIABLES
             EACH MEASURING STATION INCLUDES DATA FOR THE FOLLOWING
             MEASURED OR INFERRED QUANTITIES:
             X              STREAMWISE DISTANCE. M (MEASURED FROM AN
                            ARBITRARY POINT IN THE WIND TUNNEL)
             XM             MACH NUMBER EXTERNAL TO BOUNDARY LAYER
             THETA          MOMENTUM THICKNESS. M
             CF             SKIN FRICTION COEFFICIENT BASED ON BOUNDARY
                            LAYER EDGE CONDITIONS
             H              NONDIMENSIONAL SHAPE FACTOR (DELS/THETA)
             PSTAT          STATIC PRESSURE. N/M**2
             UE             VELOCITY EXTERNAL TO THE BOUNDARY LAYER. M/S
             Y              TRANSVERSE DISTANCE MEASURED FROM TEST SURFACE. M
             U              MEAN VELOCITY. M/S
             U/UE           NONDIMENSIONAL VELOCITY.

     10.     MEASUREMENT UNCERTAINTY
             UNCERTAINTIES ESTIMATED FROM BASIC MEASUREMENT UNCERTAINTY
             PSTAT: + OR - 0.02* PSTAT
             CF:    + OR - 0.10* CF
             U:     + OR - 0.03* U
             H:     + OR - 0.05* H

     11.     TAPE ORGANIZATION
             THE TAPE IS A 2400 FOOT, 9-TRACK, ODD PARITY, PHASE ENCODED,
             UNLABELLED TAPE WRITTEN AT A DENSITY OF 1600 BITS PER INCH
             ACCORDING TO EBCDIC CODE. THE RECORD FORMAT IS FIXED AND
             BLOCKED; RECORD LENGTH = 80 BYTES; 100 RECORDS PER BLOCK;
             BLOCKSIZE = 8000 BYTES.
             NORMALIZED DATA ARE CREATED FROM MEASURED DATA AS FOLLOWS:
             XNORM=(X-XMIN)/(XMAX-XMIN)
             THUS EACH NORMALIZED DATUM IS WRITTEN ONTO TAPE AS A
             VALUE BETWEEN 0.0 AND 1.0
             ALL NULL DATA ARE WRITTEN AS 2.0
             THE EQUATION DESCRIBING THE RELATIONSHIP BETWEEN ACTUAL
             DATA AND NORMALIZED DATA IS:
             X=XMIN + ((XMAX-XMIN) * XNORM)
             WHERE X, XMAX, XMIN AND XNORM ARE REAL NUMBERS.
