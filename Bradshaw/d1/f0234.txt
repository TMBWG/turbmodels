1.   FLOW TITLE:  CASE 0234; HUNT, I.A., AND JOUBERT, P.N.,
     EFFECTS OF SMALL STREAMLINE CURVATURE ON TURBULENT DUCT FLOW.

2.   REVISION DATE:  NOVEMBER 8, 1981

3.   EVALUATORS:  SIMON, T.W., HONAMI, S., THERMOSCIENCES DIVISION,
     DEPARTMENT OF MECHANICAL ENGINEERING, STANFORD UNIVERSITY,
     CALIFORNIA, U.S.A.

4.   EXPERIMENT LOCATION AND DATE:  UNIVERSITY OF MELBOURNE, VICTORIA,
     AUSTRALIA, 1976.

5.   ABSTRACT OF EXPERIMENT:
     MEASUREMENTS WERE MADE OF THE DEVELOPING TURBULENT FLOW IN A
  CURVED DUCT OF HIGH ASPECT RATIO RECTANGULAR CROSS-SECTION, CONSTANT
  WIDTH AND LARGE CONSTANT RADIUS OF CURVATURE.  AN OPEN-RETURN BLOWER
  TUNNEL WAS USED, WITH SCREENS AND A SETTLING SECTION UPSTREAM OF AN
  8:1, 2-D CONTRACTION BEFORE THE WORKING SECTION.  CURVATURE STARTED
  AT THE CONTRACTION EXIT/DUCT ENTRANCE PLANE, AND THE WALL BOUNDARY
  LAYERS WERE TRIPPED BY A TRANSVERSE ROW OF PIN STIMULATORS.  THE WALLS
  WERE CONSTRUCTED FROM SMOOTH-SURFACED ACRYLIC SHEETS, STIFFENED BY
  TRANSVERSE ACRYLIC BARS, AND WERE SEALED TO CURVED ALUMINIUM CHANNEL
  END WALLS.  THE MEASUREMENTS PRESENTED, WERE TAKEN AT STATIONS ALONG THE
  CENTRAL PLANE OF THE DUCT.  MEAN VELOCITY PROFILES AND STREAMWISE
  TURBULENCE INTENSITY PROFILES ARE PRESENTED FOR ALL STATIONS. TRANSVERSE
  TURBULENCE INTENSITY AND TURBULENT SHEAR STRESS PROFILES WERE ALSO GIVEN
  FOR THE FINAL STATION.  A WEAK SECONDARY FLOW STRUCTURE ATTRIBUTED TO
  TAYLOR-GOERTLER TYPE VORTICES WAS DETECTED BY SUBSIDIARY MEAN VELOCITY
  MEASUREMENTS MADE OVER THE WHOLE FLOW CROSS-SECTION AT THE DUCT EXIT.
  THESE SHOW THE AXIS OF ONE OF THE VORTICES TO BE NEARLY COINCIDENT WITH
  THE MEASUREMENT PLANE.
     THE PROFILES OF THE REYNOLDS STRESS TERMS V2 AND U1V1 WERE TAKEN
  AT THE SAME Y/D LOCATIONS.  THE PROFILES OF THE REYNOLDS STRESS TERMS
  W2 AND U1W1 WERE TAKEN AT THE SAME Y/D LOCATIONS.
     MAXIMUM VELOCITIES UMU, UMV, UMW, WALL SHEAR VELOCITIES USU, USV,
  USW AND KINEMATIC VISCOSITIES XNUU, XNUV, XNUW ARE GIVEN AS ADDITIONAL
  DATA FOR COMPLETENESS, SO THAT SCALING ASSUMPTIONS INHERENT IN THE
  USE OF AVERAGE OR NOMINAL VALUES OF THE VARIABLES CAN BE AVOIDED.

7.   INSTRUMENTATION:
     STATIC PRESSURES WERE DETERMINED FROM WALL PRESSURE TAPPINGS,
  AND A LINEAR VARIATION WAS ASSUMED TO EXIST ACROSS THE DUCT WIDTH.
  MEAN VELOCITIES WERE OBTAINED BY TRAVERSING TOTAL HEAD PROBES WORKING
  AGAINST THE WALL STATIC PRESSURE TAPPINGS.  THE PROBES WERE CALIBRATED
  AGAINST AN N.P.L.-TYPE SUB-STANDARD PITOT-STATIC TUBE.  SHEAR VELOCITIES
  WERE OBTAINED FROM THE MEAN VELOCITY PROFILES BY THE CLAUSER CHART
  METHOD, USING STRAIGHT-WALL PROCEDURES, AND AGREED WITH VALUES OBTAINED
  FROM PRESTON TUBE MEASUREMENTS.  TURBULENCE MEASUREMENTS WERE PERFORMED
  WITH AN UNLINEARIZED, CONSTANT-TEMPERATURE HOT-WIRE ANEMOMETER.  A
  SINGLE, NORMAL-WIRE PROBE WAS USED FOR THE STREAMWISE INTENSITY MEASURE-
  MENTS, AND A CROSS-WIRE PROBE WAS USED FOR THE TRANSVERSE INTENSITY
  AND TURBULENT SHEAR STRESS MEASUREMENTS.  THE ANEMOMETER SYSTEM WAS IN
  EACH CASE CALIBRATED DIRECTLY BY IMPOSING KNOWN VELOCITY PERTURBATIONS
  ON THE PROBE IN A FREE STREAM.  DETAILS OF THE ANEMOMETER AND METHODS
  OF CALIBRATION ARE GIVEN IN REFERENCES 4,5 AND 6.  PROBE WALL DISTANCE
  WAS TAKEN FROM THE TRAVERSING GAUGE.  THE TRUE ORIGIN WAS OBTAINED BY
  SIGHTING THE PROBE AND ITS REFLECTION IN THE WALL THROUGH A SHORT-RANGE
  TELESCOPE EQUIPPED WITH A GRATICULE EYEPIECE, OVER A RANGE OF PROBE
  POSITIONS.

8.   EXPERIMENTAL PARAMETERS:
     1. DUCT WIDTH (NOMINAL) D = 0.0635 M.  *
     2. MEAN RADIUS OF CURVATURE = 6.35 M = 100D .
     3. TRANSVERSE HEIGHT OF DUCT = 0.838 M = 13.2D.
     4. BOUNDARY LAYER TRIP: AT 2D FROM DUCT ENTRY.
     5. DUCT LENGTH = 4.760 M = 75D.
     6. MEASUREMENT STATIONS: AT 0D, 12D, 24D, 36D, 48D, 60D,
        AND 75D FROM DUCT ENTRY.
     7. MAXIMUM AIR VELOCITY IN CENTRAL PLANE (NOMINAL)
        UM = 14.2 M/S.    *
     8. KINEMATIC VISCOSITY (NOMINAL) XNU = 15 X 10-6 M**2/S.  *
     9. REYNOLDS NUMBER (NOMINAL) D X UM/XNU = 60000.
     *  SPECIFIC VALUES GIVEN IN DATA.

11.  TAPE ORGANIZATION:
             THE TAPE IS A 2400 FOOT, 9-TRACK, ODD PARITY, PHASE ENCODED
             UNLABELLED TAPE WRITTEN AT A DENSITY OF 1600 BITS PER INCH
             ACCORDING TO EBCDIC CODE. THE RECORD FORMAT IS FIXED AND
             BLOCKED; RECORD LENGTH = 80 BYTES; 100 RECORDS PER BLOCK;
             BLOCKSIZE = 8000 BYTES.
             NORMALIZED DATA ARE CREATED FROM MEASURED DATA AS FOLLOWS:
             XNORM=(X-XMIN)/(XMAX-XMIN)
             NORMALIZED VALUES ARE INTEGERIZED BY MULTIPLYING BY 10000
             AND ROUNDING UP OR DOWN TO THE NEAREST INTEGER.
             IXNORM = XNORM * 10000.
             THUS EACH NORMALIZED DATUM IS WRITTEN ONTO TAPE AS AN
             INTEGER VALUE BETWEEN 0 AND 10000.
             ALL NULL DATA ARE WRITTEN AS 20000
             THE EQUATION DESCRIBING THE RELATIONSHIP BETWEEN ACTUAL
             DATA AND NORMALIZED DATA IS:
             X=XMIN + (((XMAX-XMIN) * IXNORM) / 10000.)
             WHERE X, XMAX AND XMIN ARE REAL NUMBERS AND IXNORM IS INTEGER.
