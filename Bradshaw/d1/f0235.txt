             BRADSHAW, P.,
             "THE EFFECTS OF SHORT REGIONS OF HIGH SURFACE CURVATURE
             ON TURBULENT BOUNDARY LAYERS" (CONVEX 30 DEGREES).

      2.     REVISION DATE:  OCTOBER 30, 1981

      3.     EVALUATORS:  SIMON, T., HONAMI, S., STANFORD UNIVERSITY,
             STANFORD  CA  94305.

      4.     EXPERIMENT LOCATION AND DATE:  DEPARTMENT OF ASTRONAUTICS,
             IMPERIAL COLLEGE OF SCIENCE AND TECHNOLOGY, LONDON,
             ENGLAND.  1978

      5.     ABSTRACT OF EXPERIMENT:  MEASUREMENTS WERE MADE IN A
             TURBULENT BOUNDARY LAYER ON A WALL WHICH WAS JUST
             DOWNSTREAM OF A CONVEX BEND.  THE TURNING ANGLE OF THE
             BEND WAS 30 DEGREES AND THE RADIUS OF CURVATURE WAS
             0.127 M.  THE DATA SHOWS THE RESPONSE OF A
             TURBULENT BOUNDARY LAYER TO AN 'IMPULSE' OF CURVATURE.
             DATA WAS ALSO TAKEN ON BOTH CONCAVE AND CONVEX WALLS OF
             A 20 DEGREE BEND AND ON THE CONCAVE WALL OF THE 30 DEGREE
             BEND BUT WAS NOT INCLUDED IN THIS DATA SET.  THE
             BOUNDARY LAYER THICKNESS TO RADIUS OF CURVATURE RATIO AT
             THE BEGINNING OF THE BEND WAS 0.17.  THE TUNNEL HAD AN
             ASPECT RATIO OF 6.0.  THE TUNNEL HEIGHT REMAINED CONSTANT
             UPSTREAM OF, AROUND AND DOWNSTREAM OF THE BEND SO
             THE BOUNDARY LAYER EXPERIENCED ACCELERATION THEN
             DECELERATION IN AND NEAR THE BEND.  ALL DATA WAS TAKEN
             IN THE RECOVERY REGION BEGINNING APPROXIMATELY ONE
             BOUNDARY LAYER THICKNESS DOWNSTREAM OF THE BEND.

             DATA INCLUDES MEAN VELOCITY PROFILES, PROFILES OF THE 
             REYNOLDS STRESS U2, V2 AND U1V1 AND PROFILES OF THE
             THIRD ORDER QUANTITIES, U3, V3, U2V1 AND U1V2.  HIGHER
             ORDER DATA WERE TAKEN BUT NOT INCLUDED IN THE DATA SET.
             TURBULENCE DATA WERE TAKEN WITH A CONSTANT TEMPERATURE
             HOT WIRE ANEMOMETER.

             VISCOSITY FOR EACH PROFILE CAN BE CALCULATED FROM THE
             MOMENTUM THICKNESS, MOMENTUM THICKNESS REYNOLDS NUMBER AND
             THE FREESTREAM VELOCITY.

      6.     REFERENCE:  SMITS, A. J., YOUNG, S.T.B., BRADSHAW, P.,
             "THE EFFECTS OF SHORT REGIONS OF HIGH SURFACE CURVATURE
             ON TURBULENT BOUNDARY LAYERS", JFM, VOL. 94, PART 2,
             PP 209-242, DEC. 1978.

      7.     INSTRUMENTATION:
             TOTAL PRESSURE MEASUREMENTS:  TOTAL PRESSURE WAS MEASURED
             WITH A TOTAL PRESSURE TUBE OF 0.001 M DIAMETER.
             STATIC PRESSURE MEASUREMENTS:  STATIC PRESSURE PORTS ON
             THE SURFACE WERE USED.  IT WAS ASSUMED THAT THE
             STREAMLINES WERE PARALLEL.
             FLUCTUATING VELOCITY MEASUREMENTS:  HOT WIRE ANEMOMETERY
             USING A CROSS-WIRE PROBE IN A CONSTANT TEMPERATURE MODE.
             PROCESSING OF THE OUTPUT SIGNAL WAS DONE DIGITALLY.

     11.     TAPE ORGANIZATION
             THE TAPE IS A 2400 FOOT, 9-TRACK, PHASE ENCODED, ODD PARITY,
             UNLABELLED TAPE WRITTEN AT A DENSITY OF 1600 BITS PER INCH
             ACCORDING TO EBCDIC CODE. THE RECORD FORMAT IS FIXED AND
             BLOCKED; RECORD LENGTH = 80 BYTES; 100 RECORDS PER BLOCK;
             BLOCKSIZE = 8000 BYTES.
             NORMALIZED DATA ARE CREATED FROM MEASURED DATA AS FOLLOWS:
             XNORM=(X-XMIN)/(XMAX-XMIN)
             THUS EACH NORMALIZED DATUM IS WRITTEN ONTO TAPE AS A
             VALUE BETWEEN 0.0 AND 1.0
             ALL NULL DATA ARE WRITTEN AS 2.0
             THE EQUATION DESCRIBING THE RELATIONSHIP BETWEEN ACTUAL
             DATA AND NORMALIZED DATA IS:
             X=XMIN + ((XMAX-XMIN) * XNORM)
             WHERE X, XMAX, XMIN AND XNORM ARE REAL NUMBERS.
