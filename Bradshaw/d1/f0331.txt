       5.     ABSTRACT OF EXPERIMENT:
                   THE TUNNEL WAS OPERATED WITH THE WORKING SECTION ROOF
              REMOVED, THE SIDE WALLS EXTENDED IN HEIGHT, AND A VERTICAL
              PLATE 1.3 M IN HEIGHT AND 0.762 M WIDE INSTALLED 0.447 M
              DOWNSTREAM OF THE CONTRACTION EXIT.  THE TUNNEL STREAM IS
              THUS TURNED THROUGH A NOMINAL 90 DEGREES TO FLOW VERTICALLY
              UP THE PLATE, AND THE MIXING LAYER FORMED BETWEEN THE TOP
              OF THE TUNNEL STREAM AND "STILL AIR" ALSO TURNS THROUGH
              A NOMINAL 90 DEGREES.  BOUNDARY LAYER SEPARATION IN THE
              RIGHT-ANGLED CORNER BETWEEN THE FLOOR AND THE BACKPLATE
              IS PREVENTED BY ALLOWING A VOLUME OF AIR EQUAL TO A 0.04 M
              THICKNESS OF THE TUNNEL EXIT STREAM TO ESCAPE THROUGH A
              GAP IN THE CORNER.  THE SENSE OF THE CURVATURE TENDS TO
              STABILIZE THE TURBULENCE IN THE MIXING LAYER.  MEASUREMENTS
              OF MOST OF THE SECOND AND THIRD ORDER ONE-POINT MEAN PRODUCTS
              OF VELOCITY FLUCTUATIONS WERE TAKEN WITH HOT-WIRE ANEMOMETERS,
              AND PITOT PROFILES WERE ALSO MEASURED.  A FEW SPECTRA WERE
              MEASURED AND ARE PRESENTED IN REF. 1.  THE MAIN SOURCE
              OF DERIVED DATA IS REF. 2, IN WHICH RESULTS ARE PRESENTED
              IN TRANSFORMED COORDINATES TO SHOW UP DEPARTURES FROM
              SELF-PRESERVATION AS CLEARLY AS POSSIBLE.
                   THE COORDINATE SYSTEM USED FOR DEFINING POSITION AND
              RESOLVING VELOCITY IS DIFFERENT IN THE CURVED AND VERTICAL
              PORTIONS OF THE MIXING LAYER.  IN THE CURVED PORTION,
              POLAR COORDINATES ARE USED, WITH AN ORIGIN 0.126 M DOWNSTREAM
              OF THE NOZZLE EXIT AND 0.251 M ABOVE THE UPPER LIP OF THE
              NOZZLE (0.378 M ABOVE THE FLOOR).  IN THE VERTICAL PORTION,
              RECTANGULAR COORDINATES ARE USED, THE ORIGIN OF THE VERTICAL
              COORDINATE, X, BEING THE PLANE OF THE UPPER LIP OF THE
              NOZZLE WHILE THE OTHER HORIZONTAL COORDINATE, Y, IS
              MEASURED NORMAL LEFTWARD FROM THE BACKPLATE.

       7.     INSTRUMENTATION:
                   THE INSTRUMENTATION WAS CONVENTIONAL.  DISA 55D01
              CONSTANT-TEMPERATURE ANEMOMETER BRIDGES OPERATED 55F01 SINGLE
              WIRE PROBES AND 55A38 CROSS WIRE PROBES.  PROBES WERE
              INDIVIDUALLY CALIBRATED FOR RESPONSE TO MAGNITUDE AND
              DIRECTION OF VELOCITY.  THE YAW CALIBRATIONS WERE EXPRESSED
              AS THE APPARENT ANGLE BETWEEN THE WIRE AND THE STREAM,
              DEDUCED BY ASSUMING THAT THE WIRE OBEYED THE COSINE LAW:
              THIS IS A MORE ACCURATE PROCEDURE THAN INSERTING THE GEOMETRICAL
              ANGLE INTO THE COSINE LAW, AND AVOIDS ANY ASSUMPTION ABOUT
              THE "CROSSFLOW FACTOR" K.  THE ANEMOMETER OUTPUTS WERE
              RECORDED ON ANALOG MAGNETIC TAPES AND LATER TRANSFERRED
              TO DIGITAL MAGTAPE FOR PROCESSING, INCLUDING LINEARIZATION,
              ON THE MAIN COLLEGE COMPUTER.  NO SPECIAL ALLOWANCE FOR
              THE EFFECT OF HIGH TURBULENCE INTENSITY ON THE HOT WIRE
              RESPONSE HAS BEEN MADE.  MEAN VELOCITY DATA DERIVED
              FROM SINGLE HOT WIRE RESULTS AND CONTINUITY EQUATIONS,
              (SEE REFERENCE 1), ARE PRESENTED IN FILES 2 TO 19,
              CROSSED WIRE TURBULENCE DATA IN FILE 20 UNWARD.

       11.    TAPE ORGANIZATION:
                   THE TAPE IS A 2400 FOOT, 9 TRACK, ODD PARITY ,PHASE ENCODED,
              UNLABELLED TAPE WRITTEN AT A DENSITY OF 1600 BITS PER INCH
              ACCORDING TO EBCDIC CODE.  THE RECORD FORMAT IS FIXED AND
              BLOCKED; RECORD LENGTH=80 BYTES; 100 RECORDS PER BLOCK;
              BLOCKSIZE=8000 BYTES.

                   NORMALIZED DATA ARE CREATED FROM MEASURED DATA AS FOLLOWS:
              XNORM=(X-XMIN)/(XMAX-XMIN)
              NORMALIZED VALUES ARE INTEGERIZED BY MULTIPLYING BY 10000
              AND ROUNDING UP OR DOWN TO THE NEAREST INTEGER
              IXNORM=XNORM*10000
              THUS EACH NORMALIZED AND INTEGERIZED DATUM IS WRITTEN ONTO
              TAPE AS A NUMBER BETWEEN 0 AND 10000. ALL NULL DATA
              ARE WRITTEN AS 20000.  THE EQUATION DESCRIBING THE
              RELATION BETWEEN ACTUAL DATA AND THE NORMALIZED DATA
              ON TAPE IS
              X=XMIN+(((XMAX-XMIN)*IXNORM)/10000)
              WHERE X, XMAX AND XMIN ARE REAL AND IXNORM IS AN INTEGER.
