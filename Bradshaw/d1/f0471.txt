1.   FLOW TITLE:  CASE 0471;  VISWANATH, P. R., CLEARY, T. W.,
     SEEGMILLER, H. L., AND HORSTMAN, C. C.; TRAILING-EDGE FLOWS AT
     HIGH REYNOLDS NUMBER.

2.   REVISION DATE: NOVEMBER 1, 1981

3.   EVALUATOR: DRESCHER, P.;  ABTEILUNG TX-2, BROWN BOVERI & CIE
     CH-5401, BADEN, SWITZERLAND

4.   DATE AND LOCATION OF EXPERIMENT: 1978-1979, EXPERIMENTAL FLUID
     DYNAMICS BRANCH, NASA AMES RESEARCH CENTER, MOFFETT FIELD, CA.

5.   ABSTRACT: THESE MEASUREMENTS WERE MADE AS PART OF A DETAILED STUDY OF
     TRAILING-EDGE AND NEAR-WAKE FLOWS AT SUBSONIC MACH NUMBERS AND AT HIGH
     REYNOLDS NUMBERS. SYMMETRIC AND ASYMMETRIC TRAILING-EDGE FLOWS WERE STUDIED
     EACH FLOW HAVING PRESSURE GRADIENT REGIONS UPSTREAM OF THE TRAILING EDGE.
     THE MODEL CONFIGURATION WAS ESSENTIALLY A LONG FLAT-PLATE FOREBODY FOLLOWED
     BY A SHARP TRAILING-EDGE FLAP WITH AN INCLUDED ANGLE 12.5 DEG. THE BOUNDARY
     LAYER ON THE FLAT PLATE WAS TURBULENT AND FULLY DEVELOPED. EXPERIMENTS WERE
     MADE AT TWO NOMINAL FREE-STREAM MACH NUMBERS OF 0.4 AND 0.7.
     FLOW-FIELD MEASUREMENTS WERE MADE USING A LASER-DOPPLER VELOCIMETER AS WELL
     AS CONVENTIONAL PITOT AND STATIC PROBES. THE DATA INCLUDE SURFACE AND WAKE
     PRESSURE DISTRIBUTIONS, MEAN-VELOCITY, TURBULENT SHEAR STRESS AND TURBULENT
     KINETIC-ENERGY PROFILES IN THE BOUNDARY LAYER APPROACHING THE TRAILING EDGE
     AND ACROSS THE NEAR WAKE.

6.   REFERENCES: VISWANATH, P. R., CLEARY, J. W., SEEGMILLER, H. L.,
     AND HORSTMAN, C. C.: TRAILING-EDGE FLOWS AT HIGH REYNOLDS NUMBER'"
     AIAA PAPER 79-1503, JULY 1979.

7.   INSTRUMENTATION: THE EXPERIMENTS WERE CONDUCTED IN THE 0.381 M BY 0.254 M
     AMES HIGH REYNOLDS NUMBER CHANNEL BLOW-DOWN FACILITY. ALL PRESSURES WERE
     MEASURED USING STRAIN-GAUGE PRESSURE TRANSDUCERS. FLOW-FIELD MEASUREMENTS
     WERE MADE USING A TWO-COLOR LASER-DOPPLER VELOCIMETER AS WELL AS CONVENTION
     POINT AND STATIC PRESSURE PROBES.

8.   EXPERIMENTAL PARAMETERS: THE SYMMETRIC FLOWS WERE INVESTIGATED AT TWO
     NOMINAL FREE-STREAM MACH NUMBERS OF 0.4 AND 0.7: THE CORRESPONDING REYNOLDS
     NUMBERS BASED ON CHORD OF THE MODEL WERE 24.3*10**6 AND 36.6*10**6
     RESPECTIVELY.
     THE EXPERIMENTS ON THE ASYMMETRIC CASE WERE MADE AT A NOMINAL FREE-STREAM
     MACH NUMBER OF 0.4 AND AT A REYNOLDS NUMBER OF 24.3*10**6. THE FLAP WAS
     DEFLECTED DOWNWARDS BY AN ANGLE OF 6.25 DEG. SO THAT THE LOWER SURFACE OF
     THE FLAP WAS IN LINE WITH THE LOWER SURFACE OF THE FLAT PLATE. THE
     STAGNATION PRESSURE WAS 2.75X10**5 N/M**2 AND THE NOMINAL TOTAL TEMPERATURE
     WAS 266.70 K.

9.   MEASURED AND INFERRED VARIABLES:
     K        KINETIC ENERGY OF TURBULENCE
     L        MODEL LENGTH (M)
     M        NOMINAL FREE-STREAM MACH NUMBER
     P        STATIC PRESSURE (N/M**2)
     PTOT     STAGNATION PRESSURE (N/M**2)
     RE       REYNOLDS NUMBER BASED ON FREE-STREAM CONDITIONS AND MODEL CHORD
     U        MEAN-VELOCITY COMPONENT IN X DIRECTION (M/SEC)
     X        STREAMWISE COORDINATE PARALLEL TO MODEL CENTERLINE MEASURED FROM

     Y        VERTICAL COORDINATE NORMAL TO MODEL CENTERLINE MEASURED FROM THE
              MODEL SURFACE AND IN THE WAKE FROM THE MODEL TRAILING EDGE (CM)

     UREF

     TAU      TURBULENT SHEAR STRESS

11.  TAPE ORGANIZATION:
          THE TAPE IS A 2400 FOOT, ODD PARITY, PHASE ENCODED,
     UNLABELLED TAPE WRITTEN AT A DENSITY OF 1600 BITS PER INCH ACCORDING
     TO EBCDIC CODE. THE RECORD FORMAT IS FIXED AND BLOCKED; RECORD
     LENGTH = 80 BYTES; 100 RECORD PER BLOCK; BLOCKSIZE = 8000 BYTES.
          NORMALIZED DATA ARE CREATED FROM MEASURED DATA AS FOLLOWS:
     XNORM = (X - XMIN)/(XMAX - XMIN)
     ALL NULL DATA ARE WRITTEN AS 2.0
     THE EQUATION DESCRIBING THE RELATION BETWEEN ACTUAL DATA AND
     THE NORMALIZED DATA ON THE TAPE IS
     X = XMIN + ((XMAX - XMIN) * XNORM)
     WHERE X, XMAX AND XMIN AN XNORM ARE REAL.
