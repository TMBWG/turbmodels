1.  FLOW TITLE:  CASE 0142, 0143; POZZORINI, R., SIX-DEGREE CONICAL DIFFUSER
    FLOW, LOW AND HIGH CORE TURBULENCE.

2.  REVISION DATE:  NOVEMBER 8, 1981

3.  EVALUATOR:  SIMPSON, R. L., SOUTHERN METHODIST UNIVERSITY,
    DALLAS, TEXAS 75275.

4.  DATE AND LOCATION OF EXPERIMENT:  1965-1974, EIDGENOSSISCHE
    TECHNISCHE HOCHSCHULE, ZURICH.

5.  ABSTRACT:  THIS EXPERIMENT DESCRIBES THE FLOW DEVELOPMENT WITHIN
    AN AXISYMMETRIC DIFFUSER SYSTEM CONSISTING OF AN INLET PIPE
    FOLLOWED BY A STRAIGHT CONICAL DIFFUSER (INCLUDED ANGLE, 2A =
    6 DEG., AREA RATIO 4:1).  TWO CASES ARE CONSIDERED HERE; CASE 2,
    WITH LOW CORE TURBULENCE (FILES 2 - 27) AND CASE 3, WITH HIGH CORE
    TURBULENCE (FILES 28 - 39).  FREE-STREAM MEASUREMENTS WERE TAKEN
    AT VARIOUS LOCATIONS IN THE INLET PIPE AND THE DIFFUSER.  BOUNDARY
    LAYER PROFILES WERE MEASURED IN THE DIFFUSER.  THE HIGH CORE TURB-
    ULENCE IN CASE 3 WAS GENERATED BY A SUDDEN 1:4 AREA RATIO PIPE
    EXPANSION LOCATED AT THE ENTRANCE TO THE INLET PIPE.  THE SUDDEN
    EXPANSION SERVED TO GENERATE A HIGHLY TURBULENT, NEARLY HOMOGENEOUS
    FLOW CONDITION AT THE DIFFUSER ENTRANCE. THE STREAMWISE COORDINATE
    REFERENCE (X = 0) WAS TAKEN AT THE DIFFUSER ENTRANCE.
        CASE 2 IS CHARACTERIZED BY A CONVENTIONAL VELOCITY PROFILE
    WITH A FULLY TURBULENT BOUNDARY LAYER AND LOW CORE TURBULENCE REGION
    (SQRT(U2)/UE = 0.3 %).  CASE 3 IS CHARACTERIZED BY A HIGHLY TURBULENT
    FLAT VELOCITY PROFILE OF VERY GOOD ROTATIONAL SYMMETRY.

7)  INSTRUMENTATION:  FLOW DEVELOPMENT DATA WERE TAKEN BY HOT-WIRES.
    A SINGLE-WIRE, 'BOUNDARY-LAYER TYPE' PROBE WAS USED IN MEASURING
    THE MEAN STREAMWISE VELOCITY AND THE RMS-VALUE OF THE STREAMWISE
    VELOCITY FLUCTUATION, SQRT(U2), FROM WHICH THE NORMALIZED REYNOLDS
    STRESS WAS DERIVED.  MEASUREMENTS VERY NEAR THE WALL WERE MADE WITH
    THIS PROBE.  AN X-WIRE WAS USED TO MEASURE OTHER REYNOLDS STRESSES.
    THE X-WIRE WAS MINIATURE AND WAS MOUNTED ON A RIGID TRAVERSING FIXTURE
    ALLOWING A +/- 90 DEG PROBE ROTATION WHILE PROVIDING PRECISE
    ALIGNMENT OF THE PROBE AXIS WITH THE X DIRECTION.  BOTH WIRES OF THE
    X-WIRE WERE CALIBRATED SIMULTANEOUSLY.  THE GIVEN DATA CORRESPOND
    TO HOT-WIRE READINGS CORRECTED FOR THE EFFECTS OF HIGH TURBULENCE
    INTENSITY: FOR THE VARIABLES U/UI, SQRT(U2)/UI AND U2/UI**2,
    UNCORRECTED VALUES ARE ALSO INCLUDED TO COVER THE VERY-NEAR-WALL
    REGIONS AND FOR COMPARISON.

11. TAPE ORGANIZATION:
        THE TAPE IS A 2400 FOOT, 9 TRACK, ODD PARITY, PHASE ENCODED,
    UNLABELLED TAPE WRITTEN AT A DENSITY OF 1600 BITS PER INCH ACCORDING
    TO EBCDIC CODE.  THE RECORD FORMAT IS FIXED AND BLOCKED; RECORD LENGTH =
    80 BYTES; 100 RECORDS PER BLOCK; BLOCKSIZE = 8000 BYTES.

    NORMALIZED DATA ARE CREATED FROM MEASURED DATA AS FOLLOWS:
    XNORM = (X - XMIN)/(XMAX - XMIN)
    NORMALIZED VALUES ARE INTEGERIZED BY MULTIPLYING BY 10000 AND
    ROUNDING UP OR DOWN TO THE NEAREST INTEGER.
    IXNORM = XNORM * 10000.
    THUS EACH NORMALIZED DATUM IS WRITTEN ONTO TAPE AS AN INTEGER
    VALUE BETWEEN 0 AND 10000.
    ALL NULL DATA ARE WRITTEN AS 20000.
    THE EQUATION DESCRIBING THE RELATION BETWEEN ACTUAL DATA AND
    THE NORMALIZED DATA ON THE TAPE IS
    X = XMIN + (((XMAX - XMIN) * IXNORM)/10000.)
    WHERE X, XMAX, XMIN ARE REAL AND IXNORM IS AN INTEGER.

    ALL HOT-WIRE DATA ARE CORRECTED FOR THE EFFECTS OF HIGH TURBULENCE
    INTENSITY UNLESS OTHERWISE INDICATED AS (UNC).
