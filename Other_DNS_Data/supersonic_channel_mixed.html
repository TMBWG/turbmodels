DNS: Supersonic Plane-Channel Flow with One Isothermal and One Adiabatic Wall

The data on this page were provided by G. Coleman.

Direct numerical simulation is used to investigate the turbulent Prandtl number (Prt) above cold (isothermal) and hot (adiabatic) walls in a family of supersonic channel flows. A range of Reynolds numbers and mean temperature/density variations is considered. Additional details concerning the data, code, and computational methodology can be found in:

  • Lusher, D. J. and Coleman, G. N., "Numerical Study of Compressible Wall-Bounded Turbulence - the Effect of Thermal Wall Conditions on the Turbulent Prandtl Number in the Low-Supersonic Regime," International Journal of Computational Fluid Dynamics (https://www.tandfonline.com/loi/gcfd20), Published online 30 Mar 2023; doi: https://doi.org/10.1080/10618562.2023.2189247. (Contact author if you need a copy)
  • Previous reference (superseded by above reference): Lusher, D. J. and Coleman, G. N., "Numerical study of the turbulent Prandtl number in supersonic plane-channel flow - the effect of thermal boundary conditions," NASA/TM-20220010483, July 2022. https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20220010483.
