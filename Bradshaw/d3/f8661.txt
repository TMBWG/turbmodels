      1.     FLOW TITLE:  CASE 8661; PEAKE, D. J., "THREE-DIMENSIONAL
             SWEPT SHOCK/TURBULENT BOUNDARY LAYER INTERACTION".

      2.     REVISION DATE:  NOVEMBER 1, 1981

      3.     EVALUATORS:  RUBESIN, M. W. AND HORSTMAN, C. C., NASA AMES
             RESEARCH CENTER, MOFFETT FIELD CA 94035

      4.     EXPERIMENT LOCATION AND DATE:  NATIONAL AERONAUTICAL
             ESTABLISHMENT, OTTAWA, CANADA   1975

      5.     ABSTRACT OF EXPERIMENT:  EXPERIMENTALLY DETERMINED WALL
             PRESSURE DISTRIBUTIONS, LOCAL SURFACE SHEAR STRESSES AND
             THEIR DIRECTIONS, AND DETAILED TURBULENT BOUNDARY LAYER
             TRAVERSES IN NEAR ZERO HEAT TRANSFER CONDITIONS, ARE
             PRESENTED THROUGH A SKEWED SHOCK/BOUNDARY-LAYER
             INTERACTION REGION GENERATED BY A SHARP WEDGE STANDING
             NORMAL TO THE WIND TUNNEL FLOOR.  THE STUDY WAS PERFORMED
             AT A FREE-STREAM MACH NUMBER OF 2 ALONG THE WORKING
             SECTION WALL (EXTENSION OF THE FLAT NOZZLE SIDEWALL) OF
             THE NAE 5 IN X 5 IN BLOWDOWN WIND TUNNEL.  THE WEGDE
             SURFACE WAS INCLINED 8 DEGREES TO THE FREE-STREAM FLOW
             DIRECTION.  THE REYNOLDS NUMBER BASED ON THE UNDISTURBED
             TEST BOUNDARY LAYER THICKNESS WAS 1.5 * 10**5 .

      6.     REFERENCES:  1. PEAKE, D. J. "THREE-DIMENSIONAL SWEPT
             SHOCK/TURBULENT BOUNDARY LAYER SEPARATIONS WITH CONTROL
             BY AIR INJECTION", AERONAUTIC REPORT LR-592, NATIONAL
             RESEARCH COUNCIL, CANADA, JULY 1976.

      7.     INSTRUMENTATION:  STATIC PRESSURE ORIFICES AND
             THERMOCOUPLES WERE USED TO MEASURE WALL PRESSURE AND
             TEMPERATURE.  PRESTON TUBES AND SURFACE OIL FLOW WERE
             USED TO OBTAIN MAGNITUDE AND DIRECTION OF SURFACE SKIN
             FRICTION.  MEAN FLOW PROFILES WERE OBTAINED FROM A 3-TUBE
             YAW METER (COBRA PROBE) WHICH PROVIDES PITOT AND TOTAL
             TEMPERATURE AND FLOW DIRECTION IN PLANES PARALLEL TO THE
             FLAT WALL.

      8.     GLOBAL PARAMETERS
             ALL DATA WERE TAKEN AT Y = 0.0254 M.
             ANGLE OF THE WEDGE = 8 DEGREES.
             INFINITY CONDITIONS ARE DEFINED AT X = 0.00 M. (LEADING
             EDGE OF THE WEDGE).
             XMINF = 2.02
             PINF = 2.90E 04  N/M**2
             UINF = 510.0  M/S
             TEMPERATURES AT X = 0.00 M ARE
             TTOT = 23.84 DEGREES CENTIGRADE.  (296.5 DEGREES K)
             TWALL = 12.34 DEGREES CENTIGRADE.  (285.0 DEGREES K)
             TSTAT = -109.34 DEGREES CENTIGRADE. (163.5 KELVIN)
             BOUNDARY LAYER QUANTITIES AT X = 0.00 M. ARE
             DEL = 0.0051 M.
             DELS = 0.00155 M.

      9.     MEASURED VARIABLES
             EACH MEASURING STATION INCLUDES DATA FOR THE FOLLOWING
             MEASURED OR INFERRED QUANTITIES:
             X              STREAMWISE DISTANCE. M (MEASURED FROM
                            THE LEADING EDGE OF THE WEDGE).
             PWALL          WALL PRESSURE.  N/M**2
             PINF           FREE STREAM PRESSURE.  N/M**2
             PWALL/PINF     NONDIMENSIONAL PRESSURE.
             CF             SKIN FRICTION COEFFICIENT BASED ON UNDISTURBED
                            FREESTREAM CONDITIONS.
             ALPHA          FLOW ANGLE MEASURED IN THE X-Y PLANE WITH
                            RESPECT TO THE X AXIS.  DEGREES
             Z              DISTANCE MEASURED NORMAL TO THE TUNNEL FLOOR.  M
             U              MEAN VELOCITY IN X DIRECTION. M/S
             UINF           FREE STREAM VELOCITY.  M/S
             U/UINF         NONDIMENSIONAL VELOCITY.
             Y              DISTANCE MEASURED NORMAL TO BOTTOM OF THE
                            WEDGE

     10.     MEASUREMENT UNCERTAINTY
             UNCERTAINTIES ESTIMATED FROM BASIC MEASUREMENT UNCERTAINTY
             AND DATA SCATTER FROM REPEAT TESTS.
             PPITOT: + OR - 0.01 * PPITOT
             PWALL: + OR - 0.01 * PWALL
             CF:    + OR - 0.10 * CF
             ALPHA: + OR - 0.2 DEGREES
             U:     + OR - 0.02 * U
             Y:     + OR - 0.00002 M

     11.     TAPE ORGANIZATION
             THE TAPE IS A 2400 FOOT, 9-TRACK, ODD PARITY, PHASE ENCODED,
             UNLABELLED TAPE WRITTEN AT A DENSITY OF 1600 BITS PER INCH
             ACCORDING TO EBCDIC CODE. THE RECORD FORMAT IS FIXED AND
             BLOCKED; RECORD LENGTH = 80 BYTES; 100 RECORDS PER BLOCK;
             BLOCKSIZE = 8000 BYTES.
             NORMALIZED DATA ARE CREATED FROM MEASURED DATA AS FOLLOWS:
             XNORM=(X-XMIN)/(XMAX-XMIN)
             THUS EACH NORMALIZED DATUM IS WRITTEN ONTO TAPE AS A
             VALUE BETWEEN 0.0 AND 1.0
             ALL NULL DATA ARE WRITTEN AS 2.0
             THE EQUATION DESCRIBING THE RELATIONSHIP BETWEEN ACTUAL
             DATA AND NORMALIZED DATA IS:
             X=XMIN + ((XMAX-XMIN) * XNORM)
             WHERE X, XMAX, XMIN AND XNORM ARE REAL NUMBERS.
