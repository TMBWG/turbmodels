1.    FLOW TITLE: CASE 8632; DUSSAUGE, J. AND GAVIGLIO, J.;
      TURBULENT BOUNDARY-LAYER/EXPANSION INTERACTION AT SUPERSONIC SPEED.

2.    REVISION DATE: FEBRUARY 12, 1981.

3.    EVALUATOR: RUBESIN, M. W., AND HORSTMAN, C. C., NASA AMES
      RESEARCH CENTER, MOFFETT FIELD, CA. 94035.

4.    DATE AND LOCATION OF EXPERIMENT:  1977-1980. INSTITUT DE MECANIQUE
      STATISTIQUE DE LA TURBULENCE, UNIVERSITE D'AIX-MARSEILLE II
      MARSEILLE (FRANCE).

5.    ABSTRACT:  THE EXPERIMENT DEALS WITH A TURBULENT BOUNDARY LAYER
      SUBJECTED TO AN EXPANSION (INITIAL EXTERNAL MACH NUMBER
      MREF = 1.76). IT IS MADE IN A CONTINUOUS RUNNING WIND TUNNEL.
      THE EXPERIMENT IS PERFORMED ON THE FLAT BOTTOM WALL OF A CONSTANT
      WIDTH HALF-NOZZLE (SECTION AT THE SONIC NECK IS 0.17 M * 0.0656 M).
      THE BOUNDARY LAYER IS EXPANDED BY TURNING AROUND A SHARP WEDGE,
      WITHOUT SEPARATION (DEVIATION: 12 DEG.). THE STAGNATION CONDITIONS
      IN THE OUTER FLOW ARE: STAGNATION PRESSURE - PTREF = 40530 N/M**2;
      STAGNATION TEMPERATURE - TTREF = 296 DEG. K. THE INITIAL
      BOUNDARY LAYER THICKNESS AT THE SECTION X = -0.005 M IS
      DEL = 0.01; THE REYNOLDS NUMBER BASED ON EXTERNAL CONDITIONS
      AND MOMENTUM THICKNESS RE IS APPROXIMATELY 5000. THE WALL
      TEMPERATURE IS CONSTANT IN THE INTERACTION ZONE AND CORRESPONDS WITH A
      GOOD APPROXIMATION TO ADIABATIC CONDITIONS, UPSTREAM OF THE EXPANSION.
      MEAN FLOW MEASUREMENTS WERE PERFORMED WITH FLATTENED PITOT
      PROBES, STATIC PRESSURE PROBES (TYPE O.N.E.R.A. 20 K 10), HOT-WIRE
      PROBES FOR TOTAL TEMPERATURE MEASUREMENTS (METHOD: LAUFER AND
      MC CLELLAN 1956) AND THERMOCOUPLES INSERTED IN THE MODEL TO CONTROL
      THE WALL TEMPERATURE. IN THE EXPANSION ZONE, STATIC PRESSURE IS
      CALCULATED BY THE METHOD OF CHARACTERISTICS. THE DEDUCED QUANTITIES
      ARE: M, V, TEMPERATURE T, RHO. FLUCTUATION MEASUREMENTS ARE MADE
      WITH A HOT-WIRE ANEMOMETER (CONSTANT-CURRENT TYPE). THE WIRE
      TIME CONSTANT IS MEASURED IN SITU FOR EVERY POINT. VARIANCE
      MEASUREMENTS ARE CORRECTED FOR THE EFFECTS OF TIME CONSTANT
      COMPENSATION CIRCUIT IMPERFECTIONS ACCORDING TO GAVIGLIO AND
      DUSSAUGE 1977. THE MEASURED QUANTITIES INCLUDE: U1**2, THE TEMPERATURE
      T1**2 AND  THE VELOCITY TEMPERATURE CORRELATION COEFFICIENT RTU; THEY
      ARE OBTAINED BY THE KOVANZNAY FLUCTUATING DIAGRAM TECHNIQUE.
      UPSTREAM OF THE EXPANSION, MEASUREMENTS SHOW THAT THE BOUNDARY
      LAYER IS IN EQUILIBRIUM. DOWNSTREAM OF THE EXPANSION, A LARGE
      VISCOUS SUBLAYER IS OBSERVED. THE SKIN FRICTION WAS ESTIMATED
      BY CHEW'S (1978) FORMULA, BY THE VON KARMAN'S INTEGRAL EQUATION,
      BY MICHEL'S (1960) EQUILIBRIUM CORRELATION AND BY THE SLOPE OF
      THE LOG-VELOCITY PROFILES. THERE IS A CONVERGENCE OF THE DIFFERENT
      RESULTS FROM THE SECTION S = 0.07 M. HOWEVER, AT THE LAST EXPLORED
      SECTION (S = 0.113 M) THE BOUNDARY LAYER IS NOT YET IN EQUILIBRIUM.
      FLUCTUATION MEASUREMENTS IN THE EXPANSION ZONE SHOW A LARGE
      DECREASE OF U1**2 WHICH CAN BE RELATED TO DILATATION EFFECTS;
      THE "STRONG REYNOLDS ANALOGY" REMAINS VERIFIED, AND RTU IS
      PRACTICALLY UNCHANGED.

9.    MEASURED AND INFERRED VARIABLES:
      PT2       = TOTAL PRESSURE DOWNSTREAM OF A NORMAL SHOCK (PITOT PRESSURE)
      RTU       = VELOCITY TEMPERATURE CORRELATION COEFFICIENT.
      T         = TEMPERATURE DEGREES K.
      TT        = STAGNATION TEMPERATURE
      XM        = MACH NUMBER
      RHO       = DENSITY, KG/M**3
      U         = VELOCITY, M/S
      P/PTREF   = NON-DIMENSIONAL PRESSURE RATIO
      PT2/PTREF = NON-DIMENSIONAL DOWNSTREAM TOTAL PRESSURE RATIO
      PW/PTREF  = NON-DIMENSIONAL WALL PRESSURE
      S         = DISTANCE FROM THE 12 DEG WEDGE ALONG THE DEFLECTED STREAM, M
      X         = THE INITIAL STREAMWISE COORDINATE, M
      Y         = THE COORDINATE NORMAL TO SURFACE UPSTREAM OF THE DEFLECTION, M
      U1**2     = REYNOLDS STRESS
      T1**2     = TEMPERATURE VARIANCE
      Y/DEL0    = NON-DIMENSIONAL BOUNDARY LAYER THICKNESS

11.   TAPE ORGANIZATION:
           THE TAPE IS A 2400 FOOT, ODD PARITY, PHASE ENCODED,
      UNLABELLED TAPE WRITTEN AT A DENSITY OF 1600 BITS PER INCH ACCORDING
      TO EBCDIC CODE. THE RECORD FORMAT IS FIXED AND BLOCKED; RECORD
      LENGTH = 80 BYTES; 100 RECORD PER BLOCK; BLOCKSIZE = 8000 BYTES.
      NORMALIZED DATA ARE CREATED FROM MEASURED DATA AS FOLLOWS:
      XNORM = (X - XMIN)/(XMAX - XMIN)
      NORMALIZED VALUES ARE INTEGERIZED BY MULTIPLYING BY 10000 AND
      ROUNDING UP OR DOWN TO THE NEAREST INTEGER.
      IXNORM = XNORM * 10000.
      THUS EACH NORMALIZED DATUM IS WRITTEN ON TO THE TAPE AS AN INTEGER
      VALUE BETWEEN 0 AND 10000, HOWEVER THE MAXIMAS AND THE MINIMAS ARE
      WRITTEN ON TO THE TAPE AS NORMALIZED VALUES.
      ALL NULL DATA ARE WRITTEN AS 20000.
      THE EQUATION DESCRIBING THE RELATION BETWEEN ACTUAL DATA AND
      THE NORMALIZED DATA ON THE TAPE IS:
      X = XMIN + (((XMAX - XMIN) * IXNORM)/10000.)
      WHERE X, XMAX AND XMIN ARE REAL AND IXNORM IS AN INTEGER.
