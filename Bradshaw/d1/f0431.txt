 1.  FLOW TITLE:  CASE 0431;  CHEW, Y. T., SIMPSON, R. L., AND
     SHIVAPRASAD, B. G.; SEPARATING ADVERSE PRESSURE GRADIENT FLOW.

 2.  REVISION DATE:  NOVEMBER 8, 1981.

 3.  EVALUATOR:  SIMPSON, R. L., SOUTHERN METHODIST UNIVERSITY
     DALLAS, TEXAS 75275.

 4.  DATE AND LOCATION OF EXPERIMENT:  1977-79, SOUTHERN METHODIST
     UNIVERSITY, DALLAS, TEXAS. OPEN CIRCUIT BOUNDARY-LAYER TUNNEL.

 5.  ABSTRACT:  THIS EXPERIMENT WAS PERFORMED ON THE FLAT BOTTOM
     WALL OF A CONSTANT WIDTH, VARIABLE HEIGHT, CONVERGING-DIVERGING
     CHANNEL, 4.9 M LONG. MEAN FLOW AND FLUCTUATION QUANTITIES WERE
     OBTAINED FOR AN INCOMPRESSIBLE TURBULENT BOUNDARY LAYER UNDERGOING
     SEPARATION. THE BOUNDARY LAYER WAS TRIPPED BY THE 6 MM BLUNT
     LEADING EDGE OF THE TEST WALL. COMPLETE PRESSURE GRADIENT RELIEF
     WAS NOT ACHIEVED IN THE DOWNSTREAM REGION WHERE BACKFLOW NEAR
     THE WALL OCCURRED ALL OF THE TIME. THE STREAMWISE FREE-STREAM
     VELOCITY DISTRIBUTION WAS OBTAINED WITH THE NORMAL HOT-WIRE PROBE
     MOUNTED ON A CART THAT WAS TRAVERSED IN THE STREAMWISE DIRECTION.
     TO OBTAIN DUE AT A GIVEN STREAMWISE LOCATION, A QUADRATIC LEAST
     SQUARES FIT TO UPSTREAM AND DOWNSTREAM DATA WAS DIFFERENTIATED
     AND EVALUATED AT THAT LOCATION. ACTIVE SUCTION AND TANGENTIAL
     INJECTION CONTROL OF THE SIDE AND TOP WALL BOUNDARY LAYERS WAS
     USED TO PROMOTE MEAN FLOW TWO-DIMENSIONALITY. THE SKIN-FRICTION
     TERMS AND THE SUMMED MOMENTUM, PRESSURE, AND NORMAL STRESS
     TERMS OF THE INTEGRATED FORM OF THE MOMENTUM INTEGRAL EQUATON
     DIFFERED NO MORE THAN 20 PERCENT, AND DIFFERED LESS THAN 17
     PERCENT OVER 80 PERCENT OF THE LENGTH UPSTREAM OF SEPARATION.
     IN REGIONS WITHOUT INTERMITTENT BACKFLOW, THE FLOW FIELD WAS
     SURVEYED WITH HOT-WIRE ANEMOMETERS FOR U, U1U1, V1V1, AND U1V1.
     UPSTREAM OF THE REGION OF INTERMITTENT DETACHMENT, THE SURFACE
     SKIN FRICTION VALUES DEDUCED FROM VISCOUS SUBLAYER VELOCITY
     PROFILES, THE LUDWIEG-TILLMAN CORRELATION, AND THE RUBESIN, ETAL.
     (1975) SURFACE HOT-WIRE ANEMOMETER ARE IN GOOD AGREEMENT. MEAN-
     VELOCITY PROFILES NEAR SEPARATION AGREE WITH THE PERRY AND
     SCHOFIELD CORRELATION. DIRECTIONALLY SENSITIVE LASER ANEMOMETER
     DATA FOR U, V, U1U1, V1V1, -U1V1, U3, V3, U4, V4, FRACTION OF TIME
     FLOW MOVES DOWNSTREAM, GAMAU, AND FRACTION OF TIME FLOW MOVES AWAY
     FROM THE WALL, GAMAV, WERE OBTAINED WHENEVER BACKFLOW WAS PRESENT.
     GOOD AGREEMENT WITH HOT-WIRE RESULTS WAS OBTAINED WITHIN ESTIMATED
     UNCERTAINTIES IN REGIONS WHERE BOTH TECHNIQUES ARE VALID.
     CALCULATIONS SHOULD BEGIN AT 0.805 M, THE FIRST STREAMWISE LOCATION
     WHERE U, U1U1, V1V1, AND -U1V1 HOT-WIRE DATA ARE AVAILABLE. IF
     A DOWNSTREAM CONDITION IS REQUIRED FOR COMPUTATIONS, THE LASER
     AND HOT-WIRE ANEMOMETER DATA AT 4.34 M AND 4.311 M SHOULD BE USED
     FOR U, V, U1U1, V1V1, GAMAU, AND GAMAV.

 11. TAPE ORGANIZATION:
         THE TAPE IS A 2400 FOOT, 9 TRACK, ODD PARITY, PHASE ENCODED,
     UNLABELLED TAPE WRITTEN AT A DENSITY OF 1600 BITS PER INCH ACCORDING
     TO EBCDIC CODE.  THE RECORD FORMAT IS FIXED AND BLOCKED; RECORD
     LENGTH = 80 BYTES; 100 RECORDS PER BLOCK; BLOCKSIZE = 8000 BYTES.

     NORMALIZED DATA ARE CREATED FROM MEASURED DATA AS FOLLOWS:
     XNORM = (X - XMIN)/(XMAX - XMIN)
     NORMALIZED VALUES ARE INTEGERIZED BY MULTIPLYING BY 10000 AND
     ROUNDING UP OR DOWN TO THE NEAREST INTEGER.
     IXNORM = XNORM * 10000.
     THUS EACH NORMALIZED DATUM IS WRITTEN ONTO TAPE AS AN INTEGER
     VALUE BETWEN 0 AND 10000.
     ALL NULL DATA ARE WRITTEN AS 20000.
     THE EQUATION DESCRIBING THE RELATION BETWEEN ACTUAL DATA AND
     THE NORMALIZED DATA ON TAPE IS
     X = XMIN + (((XMAX - XMIN) * IXNORM)/10000.)
     WHERE X, XMAX AND XMIN ARE REAL AND IXNORM IS AN INTEGER.
