1. FLOW TITTLE: CASES 0381,0382 ; ANDREOPOULOS, J., BRADSHAW, P.,
   "MEASUREMENTS OF INTERACTING TURBULENT SHEAR LAYERS IN THE NEAR
   WAKE OF AN AIRFOIL".

2. REVISION DATE: NOVEMBER 8, 1981.

3. EVALUATOR: PATEL, V. C., INSTITUTE OF HYDRAULIC RESEARCH,
              THE UNIVERSITY OF IOWA, IOWA CITY, IOWA 52242

4. EXPERIMENT LOCATION AND DATE: IMPERIAL COLLEGE OF TECHNOLOGY,
   LONDON, ENGLAND. 1978.

5. ABSTRACT: MEASUREMENTS OF VELOCITY FLUCTUATIONS IN THE WAKE OF
   A THIN NON-LIFTING AEROFOIL ARE PRESENTED. THE FLOW IS SYMME-
   TRICAL IN CASE 0381 AND ASYMMETRICAL IN CASE 0382.  IN THE
   ASYMMETRICAL CASE THE UPPER SURFACE OF THE AIRFOIL WAS
   ROUGHENED TO INCREASE THE SURFACE SHEAR STRESS.
   MEASUREMENTS ARE CONFINED TO THE NEAR WAKE, WHERE THE DISTURBED
   REGION LIES WITHIN THE INNER LAYERS OF THE ORIGINAL BOUNDARY
   LAYERS.

   THE SYMMETRICAL CONFIGURATION IS SIMILAR TO THAT USED BY
   CHEVRAY AND KOVASZNAY (AIAA J. 7, 1641), ALTHOUGH THE REYNOLDS
   NUMBER IS TEN TIMES HIGHER IN THE PRESENT CASE.

   TURBULENCE MEASUREMENTS WERE MADE WITH DISA TYPE 55D01 CONSTANT
   TEMPERATURE ANEMOMETERS AND DISA MINIATURE CROSS-WIRE PROB
   TYPE 55A38 WITH 5 MICRON PLATINUM WIRES.
   ALL PRODUCTS OF VELOCITY FLUCTUATIONS U AND V AND TEMPERATURE
   FLUCTUATION UP TO THIRD ORDER WERE EVALUATED BY THE ANALYSIS
   PROGRAM AFTER ANALOG-TO-DIGITAL CONVERSION.

   CHECKS FOR TWO-DIMENSIALITY WERE MADE AT THE TRAILING EDGE
   AND THE MOST DOWNSTREAM STATION.  PITOT TRAVERSES INDICATED
   TWO-DIMENSIONAL FLOW WITHIN 1 TO 2% OF THE MEAN-VELOCITY
   PROFILES OVER 70% OF THE PLATE SPAN AT THE TRAILING EDGE,
   AND 50% OF THE TUNNEL WIDTH AT X = 500 MM.

6. REFERENCES: ANDREOPOULOS, J., 1978, "SYMMETRICAL ASYMMETRICAL
   TURBULENT NEAR WAKES". PHD THESIS, IMPERIAL COLLEGE OF
   TECHNOLOGY, LONDON. (AVAILABLE IN MICROFICHE).

7. INSTRUMENTATION: DISA CONSTANT TEMPERATURE ANEMOMETERS WITH
   AMPEX FRI-300 ANALOG TAPE RECORDER.   THE SIGNAL WAS PLAYED BACK
   AT ONE FOURTH OF THE RECORDING SPEED AND DIGITIZED.

8. EXPERIMENTAL PARAMETERS:
   WIND TUNNEL DIMENSIONS:...................91 CM X 91 CM
   REFERENCE FREE-STREAM VELOCITY:...........33 M/S
   FREE-STREAM TURBULENCE LEVELS:............LESS THAN 0.05%.
   FLAT PLATE LENGTH:........................3.08 M
   FLAT PLATE THICKNESS:.....................0.028 M.

   THE LEADING EDGE, 80 MM LONG, WAS BASED ON THE NACA 0009 SECTION.
   THE REAR 450 MM LENGTH WAS TAPERED TO YIELD A SHARP TRAILING EDGE
   INCLUDED TE ANGLE 3.5 DEG. AND TE THICKNESS OF 0.07 MM.
   THE BOUNDARY LAYER WAS TRIPPED BY MEANS OF 50 MM WIDE SAND PAPER
   STRIPS JUST DOWNSTREAM OF THE 80 MM LEADING EDGE.


9. MEASURED VARIABLES:

   SYMMETRIC CASE 0381:
        MEAN-VELOCITY PROFILES AT 10 STREAMWISE STATIONS:
             X = 0, 5, 10, 30, 50, 100, 200, 300, AND 500 MM.
        REYNOLDS STRESSES U**2, V**2, UV AT 4 STATIONS:
             X = 50, 100, 400 MM.
        TRIPLE CORRELATIONS U**3, UV**2, U**2V, V**3 AT THE ABOVE
             4 STATIONS.
        CONDITIONAL AVERAGES TO STUDY THE MIXING IN THE CENTRAL PART
             OF THE WAKE.

   ASYMMETRIC CASE 0382:
        MEAN-VELOCITY  PROFILES AT 6 STREAMWISE STATIONS:
             X = 0, 25, 50, 100, 200, AND 400 MM.
        REYNOLDS STRESSES U**2, V**2, UV AT 4 STATIONS:
             X = 25, 100, AND 400 MM.
        TRIPLE CORRELATIONS U**3, U**2V, UV**2, V**3 AT THE ABOVE
             4 STATIONS.
        INTERMITTENCY.
        CONDITIONAL AVERAGES TO STUDY THE MIXING IN THE CENTRAL
             PART OF THE WAKE.

10. UNCERTAINTY:  NONE REPORTED.

11. TAPE ORGANIZATION: THE TAPE IS A 2400 FOOT, 9 TRACK, ODD PARITY,
    PHASE ENCODED, UNLABELLED TAPE WRITTEN AT A DENSITY OF 1600 BITS
    PER INCH ACCORDING TO EBCDIC CODE.  THE RECORD FORMAT IS FIXED AND
    BLOCKED; RECORD LENGTH = 80 BYTES; BLOCKSIZE = 8000 BYTES

    NORMALIZED DATA ARE CREATED FROM MEASURED DATA AS FOLLOWS:
    XNORM = (X - XMIN)/(XMAX - XMIN)
    NORMALIZED VALUES ARE INTEGERIZED BY MULTIPLYING BY 10000 AND
    ROUNDING UP OR DOWN TO THE NEAREST INTEGER.
    IXNORM = XNORM * 10000.
    THUS EACH NORMALIZED DATUM IS WRITTEN ONTO TAPE AS AN INTEGER
    VALUE BETWEEN 0 AND 10000.
    ALL NULL DATA ARE WRITTEN AS 20000
    THE EQUATION DESCRIBING THE RELATION BETWEEN ACTUAL DATA AND
    THE NORMALIZED DATA ON TAPE IS
    X = XMIN + (((XMAX - XMIN) * IXNORM)/10000.)
    WHERE X, XMAX AND XMIN ARE REAL AND IXNORM IS INTEGER.
