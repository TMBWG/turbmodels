1.  FLOW TITLE: CASE 0361;  CHEVRAY, R., "THE TURBULENT WAKE OF A BODY OF
    REVOLUTION"

2.  REVISION DATE:  FEBRUARY 8, 1981

3.  EVALUATOR:  PATEL, V. C.; INSTITUTE OF HYDRAULIC RESEARCH, THE
    UNIVERSITY OF IOWA, IOWA CITY, IOWA 52242.

4.  DATE AND LOCATION OF EXPERIMENT:  1968, INSTITUTE OF HYDRAULIC
    RESEARCH, THE UNIVERSITY OF IOWA.

5.  ABSTRACT:  THE EXPERIMENT WAS CONDUCTED IN THE LARGE CLOSED-CIRCUIT
    WIND TUNNEL OF THE IOWA INSTITUTE OF HYDRAULIC RESEARCH.  THE WORKING
    SECTION WAS 7.32 M. LONG WITH A CROSS-SECTION IN THE FORM OF A 1.52
    M. OCTAGON.  RECENT MEASUREMENTS IN THE TUNNEL INDICATE THAT THE
    FREE-STREAM TURBULENCE LEVEL IS OF THE ORDER OF 0.2%.
    THE MODEL WAS A SMOOTH 6:1, 1.52 M. LONG, 0.254 M. MAXIMUM DIA-
    METER SPHEROID.  THE PRESSURE DISTRIBUTION ON THE MODEL SURFACE WAS
    NOT MEASURED.  THE RECENT MEASUREMENTS OF PATEL ET AL U3,4e ON
    MODELS OF SIMILAR DIMENSIONS IN THE SAME WIND TUNNEL INDICATE, HOW-
    EVER, THAT THE TUNNEL BLOCKAGE EFFECTS WERE NEGLIGIBLE.  CONSEQUENT-
    LY, THE POTENTIAL-FLOW SOLUTION MAY BE USED, IF REQUIRED, FOR THE
    REGION BETWEEN THE NOSE AND A SHORT DISTANCE UPSTREAM OF THE OB-
    SERVED SEPARATION POINT AT X/D = -0.12.  THE BOUNDARY LAYER ON THE
    BODY WAS TRIPPED ARTIFICIALLY AT A DISTANCE 0.3 D FROM THE NOSE.
    ALL TESTS WERE CONDUCTED AT A LENGTH REYNOLDS NUMBER, UI*L/XNU =
    2.75E 06, WHICH CORRESPONDS TO A TUNNEL VELOCITY OF APPROXIMATELY
    27.4 M/S.  UNDER THESE CONDITIONS, THE ZONE OF SEPARATED FLOW, AS
    DETERMINED BY VISUAL STUDIES, EXTEND OVER -0.2 < X/DREF < 0.10 OR
    -0.02 < X/LREF < 0.017 (DREF = 0.254 M., LREF = 1.52 M.)

7.  INSTRUMENTATION:  STATIC PRESSURE VARIATIONS ACROSS THE BOUNDARY
    LAYER WERE MEASURED BY MEANS OF A STANDARD STATIC PROBE ALIGNED
    IN THE FLOW DIRECTION.  ALL MEASURMENTS OF MEAN VELOCITIES AND
    REYNOLDS STRESSES WERE MADE WITH LINEARIZED HOT-WIRE ANEMOMETRY.
    THE MEASUREMENTS INCLUDE THE AXIAL COMPONENT OF MEAN VELOCITY,
    U, THE AXIAL, U2, RADIAL, V2, AND CIRCUMFERENTIAL, W2, REYNOLDS
    NORMAL STRESSES AND THE SHEAR STRESS, UV, AT 12 AXIAL STATIONS
    IN THE RANGE -0.25 < X/DREF < 18.0.  THUS, THE MEASUREMENTS IN THE
    BOUNDARY LAYER AT X/DREF = -0.25 DO NOT CORRESPOND TO THE USUAL
    BOUNDARY LAYER COORDINATES.  SINCE, AT THIS STATION, THE ANGLE BE-
    TWEEN THE SURFACE TANGENT AND THE AXIS IS 21 DEG. AND THE RATIO
    DEL/R0 IS 1.0, WHERE R0 IS THE LOCAL TRANSVERSE RADIUS OF THE BODY,
    THE DATA MAY NEED CORRECTION IF THEY ARE TO BE USED FOR COMPARISON
    WITH BOUNDARY LAYER CALCULATIONS.
