High Fidelity CFD Workshop 2022

Associated with AIAA SciTech 2022

Dates: January 8-9, 2022
 
 

The information on this page describes the workshop held in 2022. The next iteration of this workshop has a slightly different name (High-Fidelity CFD Verification Workshop) and a new site. Please go to:

https://HighFidelityCFDVerificationWorkshop.github.io
 
 

This workshop, focused on exploring high-fidelity CFD methodologies, is made up of 5 different test suites. Note: a preparatory High Fidelity CFD Pre-Workshop Special Session was held at AIAA SciTech 2021. This workshop is a continuation of a previous series known as "International Workshop on High Order CFD Methods", or "HiOCFD". See, for example: HiOCFD5 and HiOCFD4.

Registration: Link to AIAA registration site

Workshop Agenda:

  • Workshop_Agenda (pdf file) (updated on 01/06/2022)

Test suite presentation format:

  • Test suite lead will introduce the test case(s)
  • Individual participant presentations (~10 minutes each)
    • Intent is to communicate approach and conclusions, NOT to present results (test suite lead will present results)
    • See Presentation guidelines (pdf file) and OPTIONAL Presentation Template (pptx file)
    • Virtual participants: please provide your presentation charts and optionally pre-recorded presentation (as backup in-case of technical difficulty) by THURSDAY 6 JANUARY 2022
  • Test suite lead will present collected results from all participants
  • Group discussion

Please be sure to contact the individual test-suite leads to coordinate individual presentations and for any test-suite-specific guidance on individual presentations.

Mission:

  • Providing an open and impartial forum for evaluating the status of CFD methods in solving a wide range of flow problems;
  • Assessing the performance of CFD methods through comparison to production CFD codes widely used in the aerospace industry with well-defined metrics;
  • Supporting new research in the field of high order CFD methods by providing validation cases and aid during development and improvement of codes;
  • Establish best practices for large scale simulations

Vision:

  • Identifying pacing items in Solver Technology, LES, and High-Order methods and peripheral tools that need additional research and development in order to proliferate in the CFD community;
  • Facilitating international collaboration and enhance mutual understanding among CFD researchers and managers in academia, government, and industries
