      1.     FLOW TITLE:  CASE 0233;  GILLIS, J. C., JOHNSTON, J. P.
             "TURBULENT BOUNDARY LAYER ON A CONVEX, CURVED SURFACE".

      2.     REVISION DATE:  NOVEMBER 18, 1980

      3.     EVALUATORS:  SIMON, T., HONAMI, S., STANFORD UNIVERSITY,
             STANFORD  CA  94305

      4.     EXPERIMENT LOCATION AND DATE:  DEPARTMENT OF MECHANICAL
             ENGINEERING, STANFORD UNIVERSITY, STANFORD, JUNE 1980

      5.     ABSTRACT OF EXPERIMENT:  MEASUREMENTS WERE MADE IN A
             TURBULENT BOUNDARY LAYER ON A WALL WHICH HAD STRONG
             CONVEX CURVATURE.  THE RADIUS OF CURVATURE WAS 0.45 M
             AND THE RATIO OF BOUNDARY LAYER THICKNESS TO RADIUS OF
             CURVATURE AT THE BEGINNING OF THE CURVE WAS 0.10.
             SECONDARY FLOWS WERE HELD TO A MINIMUM USING SIDEWALL SUCTION
             AND FENCES MOUNTED ON THE TEST WALL.  THE TUNNEL HAD AN ASPECT
             RATIO OF 2.8; THE ASPECT RATIO DEFINED AS THE FENCE HEIGHT
             DIVIDED BY DISTANCE BETWEEN FENCES WAS 12.0.  YAW MEASUREMENTS
             SHOWED THAT THE CONVERGENCE ANGLES WERE LESS THAN 2.5 DEGREES.
             THE TEST WALL STATIC PRESSURE WAS MAINTAINED CONSTANT IN THE
             STREAMWISE DIRECTION BY ADJUSTING THE OUTER (CONCAVE) WALL;
             SUCTION WAS APPLIED TO THE CONCAVE WALL AT THE START OF
             CURVATURE TO AVOID SEPARATION AND LOSS OF STATIC PRESSURE
             CONTROL.

             DATA TAKEN INCLUDE MEAN VELOCITY PROFILES AND PROFILES OF
             THE REYNOLDS STRESS COMPONENTS U1V1, U2, V2 AND W2.
             TURBULENCE DATA WERE TAKEN USING HOT WIRE ANEMOMETRY
             USING A ROTATABLE X-WIRE PROBE IN A CONSTANT TEMPERATURE
             MODE WITH ANALOG PROCESSING OF THE SIGNAL.

             THE LOCAL STATIC PRESSURE USED IN THE MEAN VELOCITY
             MEASUREMENT WAS DEDUCED FROM THE WALL STATIC PRESSURE BY
             USING THE EULER N-EQUATION ASSUMING POTENTIAL FLOW:
             (PTOT - PSTAT)/(PTOT - PSW) = (RAD/(RAD + N)) ** 2  WHERE
             PTOT IS THE LOCAL STAGNATION PRESSURE, PSTAT IS THE LOCAL
             STATIC PRESSURE, PSW IS THE WALL STATIC PRESSURE, RAD
             IS THE RADIUS OF CURVATURE OF THE TEST WALL AND N IS THE
             DISTANCE NORMAL TO THE TEST WALL.  THEN :
             U ** 2 = 2 * (PTOT - PSTAT) / RHO.

             VISCOSITY FOR EACH PROFILE CAN BE CALCULATED FROM THE
             MOMENTUM THICKNESS, MOMENTUM THICKNESS REYNOLDS NUMBER AND
             THE POTENTIAL VELOCITY EXTRAPOLATED TO THE WALL (UPW) :
             RTHETA = THETA * UPW / XNU

      7.     INSTRUMENTATION
             MEAN VELOCITY MEASUREMENTS:  TOTAL PRESSURE WAS MEASURED
             WITH A PITOT TUBE OF 0.71 M.M. OUTER DIAMETER.
             STATIC PRESSURE MEASUREMENTS:  STATIC PRESSURE PORTS IN
             THE TEST WALL WITHIN THE BEND AND IN THE SIDEWALL
             UPSTREAM AND DOWNSTREAM OF THE BEND WERE USED.
             FLUCTUATING VELOCITY MEASUREMENTS:  HOT WIRE ANEMOMETERY
             USING AN X-WIRE IN A CONSTANT TEMPERATURE MODE. SIGNAL
             PROCESSING (LINEARIZATION AND TIME-AVERAGING) WAS DONE
             WITH ANALOG EQUIPMENT.  HORIZONTAL WIRE DATA WERE ALSO
             TAKEN BUT NOT INCLUDED IN THIS DATA SET.

             BLOCKSIZE = 8000 BYTES.
             NORMALIZED DATA ARE CREATED FROM MEASURED DATA AS FOLLOWS:

             DATA AND NORMALIZED DATA IS:
             X=XMIN + ((XMAX-XMIN) * XNORM)
