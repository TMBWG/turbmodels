 5. ABSTRACT: TWENTY-THREE EQUILIBRIUM VELOCITY PROFILES WERE
    MEASURED FOR A STANDARD FLAT PLATE BOUNDARY LAYER.  THE
    MEASUREMENTS HAVE BEEN CHOSEN AS TYPICAL OF THE FLAT PLATE
    BOUNDARY LAYER, WITH CERTAIN RESERVATIONS.  THE MODEL WAS A
    WAXED PLYWOOD PLATE WITH A SLIGHTLY UNDULATING SURFACE; THE
    TRIPPING DEVICE WAS A BLUNT LEADING EDGE FITTED WITH A SMALL
    TRIP WIRE.  THE FIRST PROFILE IS PROBABLY AT ABOUT THE MINIMUM
    REYNOLDS NUMBER FOR TURBULENT FLOW.  THE DATA WERE OBTAINED
    USING A RAKE RATHER THAN A SINGLE PROBE.  THIS TECHNIQUE FIXES
    THE RELATIVE POSITION OF THE DATA POINTS AND AVOIDS SOME OF
    THE EFFECTS OF DRIFT IN TUNNEL CONDITIONS BY SPEEDING UP THE
    DATA-TAKING PROCESS.  HOWEVER, THE PROFILES MAY BE SUBJECT TO
    MORE WALL INTERFERENCE THAN USUAL.  MOREOVER, THE PROFILES ARE
    DEFINED BY A RELATIVELY SMALL NUMBER OF POINTS, NONE OF WHICH
    LIE IN THE SUBLAYER.  CONSEQUENTLY, COMPUTED VALUES FOR THE
    INTEGRAL PARAMETERS (ESPECIALLY FOR THE FIRST FEW STATIONS)
    ARE STRONGLY AFFECTED BY THE CHOICE OF INTERPOLATION SCHEME
    FOR THE INTEGRATION.

 9. MEASURED VARIABLES:
    VELOCITY EXTERNAL TO THE BOUNDARY LAYER (UE)
    STREAMWISE MEAN VELOCITY (U)
    DISTANCE NORMAL TO THE PLATE (Y)
    DISTANCE FROM THE LEADING EDGE (X)

    EACH MEASURING STATION INCLUDES DATA FOR THE FOLLOWING
    MEASURED OR INFERRED QUANTITIES
    X            DISTANCE MEASURED FROM LEADING EDGE IN M
    US           WALL SHEAR VELOCITY IN M/S
    UE           VELOCITY EXTERNAL TO THE BOUNDARY LAYER IN M/S
    THETA        MOMENTUM THICKNESS IN M
    DELS         DISPLACEMENT THICKNESS IN M
    ENTH         ENERGY THICKNESS IN M
    CLTH         CLAUSER THICKNESS IN M
    H            NONDIMENSIONAL SHAPE FACTOR (DELS/THETA)
    G            NONDIMENSIONAL EQUILIBRIUM SHAPE FACTOR
    HS           NONDIMENSIONAL SHAPE FACTOR (ENTH/THETA)
    RTHETA       REYNOLDS NUMBER BASED ON THETA
    RDELS        REYNOLDS NUMBER BASED ON DELS
    DUE          STREAMWISE GRADIENT OF VELOCITY EXTERNAL TO
                 BOUNDARY LAYER IN (S** -1)
    CF           NONDIMENSIONAL SKIN FRICTION COEFFICIENT
    CFLT         NONDIMENSIONAL SKIN FRICTION COEFFICIENT ACCORDING
                 TO LUDWIEG-TILLMANN FORMULA
    CFE          NONDIMESIONAL SKIN FRICTION COEFFICIENT AS REPORTED
                 BY THE ORIGINATOR
    BETA         NONDIMENSIONAL EQUILIBRIUM PARAMETER
    YPLUS        NONDIMENSIONAL WALL VARIABLE
    UPLUS        NONDIMENSIONAL WALL VARIABLE
    Y            DISTANCE MEASURED NORMAL TO PLATE IN M
    U/UE         NONDIMESIONAL MEAN VELOCITY
    Y/CLTH       DISTANCE MEASURED FROM PLATE NONDIMENSIONALIZED
                 WITH RESPECT TO CLAUSER THICKNESS (COLES & HIRST
                 USE Y/CD)
    UDEF         NONDIMENSIONAL DEFECT VELOCITY

10. UNCERTAINTY: NONE SPECIFIED

11. TAPE ORGANIZATION: THE TAPE IS A 2400 FOOT, 9 TRACK, ODD PARITY,
    PHASE ENCODED, UNLABELLED TAPE WRITTEN AT A DENSITY OF 1600 BITS
    PER INCH ACCORDING TO EBCDIC CODE.  THE RECORD FORMAT IS FIXED AND
    BLOCKED; RECORD LENGTH = 80 BYTES; BLOCKSIZE = 8000 BYTES

    NORMALIZED DATA ARE CREATED FROM MEASURED DATA AS FOLLOWS:
    XNORM = (X - XMIN)/(XMAX - XMIN)
    NORMALIZED VALUES ARE INTEGERIZED BY MULTIPLYING BY 10000 AND
    ROUNDING UP OR DOWN TO THE NEAREST INTEGER.
    IXNORM = XNORM * 10000.
    THUS EACH NORMALIZED DATUM IS WRITTEN ONTO TAPE AS AN INTEGER
    VALUE BETWEEN 0 AND 10000.
    ALL NULL DATA ARE WRITTEN AS 20000
    THE EQUATION DESCRIBING THE RELATION BETWEEN ACTUAL DATA AND
    THE NORMALIZED DATA ON TAPE IS
    X = XMIN + (((XMAX - XMIN) * IXNORM)/10000.)
    WHERE X, XMAX AND XMIN ARE REAL AND IXNORM IS INTEGER.
