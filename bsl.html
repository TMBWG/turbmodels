Note: the term in the Boussinesq approximation for tau_ij is sometimes ignored in the momentum and energy equations. Similarly, the production term in two-equation turbulence models is often approximated by P = mu_t S2. This expression is exact for incompressible flows and is typically considered a very good approximation, except perhaps for very high Mach number flows (see items 4 and 7 on the page Notes on Running the Cases with CFD, and the Implementing Turbulence Models into the Compressible RANS Equations page). There are various ways that these approximations can be implemented:

  • (BSLm) (most common) - when the term is IGNORED in tau_ij in the momentum and energy equations, and the production term is APPROXIMATED by P = mu_t S2.
  • (BSLs) - when the term is INCLUDED in tau_ij in the momentum and energy equations, and the production term is APPROXIMATED by P = mu_t S2.
  • (BSLe) - when the term is IGNORED in tau_ij in the momentum and energy equations, and the production term is THE EXACT expression.

 

Menter Baseline Two-Equation Model with Vorticity Source Term (BSL-V) and (BSL-Vm)

This form of the BSL model is sometimes used because vorticity magnitude is usually readily available in most Navier-Stokes codes. Furthermore, the vorticity source term is often nearly identical to the exact source term in simple boundary layer flows, and the use of the vorticity term can avoid some numerical difficulties sometimes associated with the use of the exact source term. The reference for this usage is:

  • Menter, F. R., "Improved Two-Equation k-omega Turbulence Models for Aerodynamic Flows," NASA TM 103975, October 1992, https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/19930013620.
