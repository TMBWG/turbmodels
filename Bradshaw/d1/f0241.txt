1.  FLOW TITLE:  CASE 0241;  ANDERSEN, P. S., KAYS, W. M., AND MOFFAT
    ,R. J.;  ZERO PRESSURE GRADIENT CONSTANT INJECTION.

2.  REVISION DATE:  NOVEMBER 1, 1981

3.  EVALUATOR:  SQUIRE, L. C., CAMBRIDGE UNIVERSITY ENGINEERING
    DEPARTMENT, TRUMPINGTON STREET, CAMBRIDGE CB2 1PZ, ENGLAND.

4.  DATE AND LOCATION OF EXPERIMENT:  1971, STANFORD UNIVERSITY,
    DEPARTMENT OF MECHANICAL ENGINEERING.

5.  ABSTRACT:  THIS EXPERIMENTAL PROGRAM WAS CARRIED OUT ON THE STANFORD
    UNIVERSITY HEAT AND MASS TRANSFER APPARATUS WHICH ALLOWS ACCURATE
    CONTROL OF THE LONGITUDINAL PRESSURE GRADIENT AND OF THE INJECTION
    MASS FLOW RATE.  THE INVESTIGATION WAS LIMITED TO LOW SPEEDS (U =
    10 M/S) WITH AIR AS THE TRANSPIRATION FLUID.  IN THE MAIN THE RESULTS
    FOR VELOCITY PROFILES WERE OBTAINED BY USE OF CONSTANT TEMPERATURE
    HOT WIRES AND AN IMPORTANT FEATURE OF THE WORK WAS THE MEASUREMENT
    OF SKIN FRICTION.  THIS WAS OBTAINED BY MEASURING THE REYNOLDS
    STRESS AT A POINT CLOSE TO THE WALL AND THEN USING MEAN FLOW VEL-
    OCITIES BETWEEN THAT POINT AND THE WALL, TOGETHER WITH THE EQUATIONS
    OF MOTION, TO EVALUATE THE WALL SKIN FRICTION.

    RESULTS WERE OBTAINED FOR A WIDE RANGE OF INJECTION AND SUCTION RATES
    IN A SERIES OF EQUILIBRIUM LAYERS.  CHECKS BY THE OVERALL MOMENTUM
    INTEGRAL EQUATION HAVE SHOWN THAT THE LAYERS ARE CLOSELY TWO-
    DIMENSIONAL.  THIS TEST CASE WAS CHOSEN AS A BASIC DATA SET FOR
    INJECTION WITHOUT THE COMPLICATION OF PRESSURE GRADIENT, YET
    WITH AN INJECTION RATE WHICH WAS SUFFICIENTLY LARGE TO PRODUCE A
    SIGNIFICANT EFFECT ON THE BOUNDARY LAYER PROFILES.

6.  REFERENCES:  ANDERSEN, P. S., KAYS, W. M., AND MOFFAT, R. J.;
    THE TURBULENT BOUNDARY ON A POROUS PLATE: AN EXPERIMENTAL STUDY OF THE
    FLUID MECHANICS FOR ADVERSE FREE-STREAM PRESSURE GRADIENTS.
    THERMOSCIENCES DIVISION, DEPARTMENT OF MECHANICAL ENGINEERING,
    STANFORD UNIVERSITY.  REPORT # HMT-15, 1972.

7.  INSTRUMENTATION:  MEAN FLOW MEASUREMENTS WERE OBTAINED BY USE OF
    A CONVENTIONAL CONSTANT TEMPERATURE HOT WIRE WHICH WAS ALSO USED
    TO MEASURE U1U1.  A ROTATABLE 45 DEGREE SLANT HOT WIRE WAS USED
    FOR THE MEASUREMENT OF -V1U1 AND (IN CONJUNCTION WITH THE HORIZONTAL
    WIRE) V1V1 AND W1W1.

8.  EXPERIMENTAL PARAMETERS:  THE TESTS WERE CONDUCTED AT VELOCITIES
    OF ABOUT 10 M/S IN A TEST SECTION 2.3 M LONG AND 0.5 M WIDE.  THE
    ACTUAL HEIGHT WAS ABOUT 0.15 M.  THE TEST SURFACE CONSISTED OF 24
    SECTIONS OF POROUS PLATES WITH A SEPARATE MASS-FLOW CONTROL ON EACH.
    THE PLATES WERE 0.006 M THICK AND WERE MADE OF SINTERED BRONZE
    MATERIAL COMPOSED OF PARTICLES WITH DIAMETERS BETWEEN 0.000058 M
    AND 0.000018 M.  THE POROSITY AND FLOW RATE UNIFORMITY IS STATED
    TO BE WITHIN +/- 6%, KINEMATIC VISCOSITY, XNU = 1.54 E-5 METER**2/SECOND.

9.  MEASURED AND INFERRED VARIABLES:

      X     STREAMWISE COORDINATE, M.
      UE    VELOCITY EXTERNAL TO THE BOUNDARY LAYER, M/S.
      F     INJECTION FLOW RATE.
      THETA MOMENTUM THICKNESS, M.
      DELS  DISPLACEMENT THICKNESS, M.
      CF    SKIN FRICTION COEFFICIENT.
      Y     TRANSVERSE COORDINATE, M.
      U/UE  NON-DIMENSIONAL STREAMWISE VELOCITY.

10. MEASUREMENT UNCERTAINTIES:

      X     -/+ 0.001
      UE    +/- 0.0375
      F     +/- 6%
      THETA +/- 0.0001
      DELS  +/- 0.0001
      CF    +/- 0.0001
      Y     +/- 0.254E -5
      U/UE  +/- 0.0375

11. TAPE ORGANIZATION:
        THE TAPE IS A 2400 FOOT, 9 TRACK, ODD PARITY, PHASE ENCODED UNLABELED
    TAPE WRITTEN AT A DENSITY OF 1600 BITS PER INCH ACCORDING TO EBCDIC
    CODE.  THE RECORD FORMAT IS FIXED AND BLOCKED; RECORD LENGTH =
    80 BYTES; 100 RECORDS PER BLOCK; BLOCKSIZE = 8000 BYTES.

    NORMALIZED DATA ARE CREATED FROM MEASURED DATA AS FOLLOWS:
    XNORM = (X - XMIN)/(XMAX - XMIN)
    NORMALIZED VALUES ARE INTEGERIZED BY MULTIPLYING BY 10000 AND
    ROUNDING UP OR DOWN TO THE NEAREST INTEGER.
    IXNORM = XNORM * 10000.
    THUS EACH NORMALIZED DATUM IS WRITTEN ONTO THE TAPE AS AN INTEGER
    VALUE BETWEEN 0 AND 10000.
    ALL NULL DATA ARE WRITTEN AS 20000.
    THE EQUATION DESCRIBING THE RELATION BETWEEN ACTUAL DATA AND
    THE NORMALIZED DATA ON THE TAPE IS
    X = XMIN + (((XMAX - XMIN) * IXNORM)/10000.)
    WHERE X, XMAX AND XMIN ARE REAL AND IXNORM IS AN INTEGER.
