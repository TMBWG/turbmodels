1.  FLOW TITLE:  CASE 0141; SAMUEL, A. E. AND JOUBERT, P. N.;
    INCREASINGLY ADVERSE PRESSURE GRADIENT FLOW.

2.  REVISION DATE:  NOVEMBER 6, 1981

3.  EVALUATOR:  SIMPSON, R. L., SOUTHERN METHODIST UNIVERSITY,
    DALLAS, TEXAS 75275.

4.  DATE AND LOCATION OF EXPERIMENT:  1970-1971; UNIVERSITY OF MELBOURNE,
    AUSTRALIA,  RETURN-CIRCUIT BOUNDARY LAYER WIND TUNNEL.

5.  ABSTRACT:  THIS EXPERIMENT WAS PERFORMED IN A 2.5 M. LONG REGION OF
    A FLEXIBLE-ROOF TUNNEL WITH A FINELY CONTROLLED STREAMWISE PRESSURE
    DISTRIBUTION.  MEAN FLOW AND FLUCTUATING QUANTITIES WERE OBTAINED
    IN AN INCOMPRESSIBLE TURBULENT BOUNDARY LAYER DEVELOPING ON A SMOOTH
    WALL IN A PRESSURE DOMAIN WHERE BOTH DP/DX AND D2P/DX2 WERE POSITIVE (IN-
    CREASINGLY ADVERSE PRESSURE GRADIENT).  THE BOUNDARY LAYER WAS TRIPPED BY
    0.032 M. DIAMETER PINS, 0.025 M. HIGH, IN A ROW, WITH A 0.064 M. CENTER
    SPACING AT A STREAMWISE POSITION OF X = 0.161 M.  THIS FLOW HAS A STRONG
    ENOUGH PRESSURE GRADIENT THAT IT APPEARS TO BE APPROACHING SEPARATION AT
    THE END OF THE WORKING REGION, BUT NO SEPARATION IS PRESENT.  THE TWO-
    DIMENSIONAL NATURE OF THE FLOW FIELD WAS CHECKED BY A MOMENTUM BALANCE
    USING VELOCITY TRAVERSES ON EITHER SIDE OF THE WORKING SECTION CENTERLINE.
    THE SKIN-FRICTION TERM AND THE SUMMED MOMENTUM AND PRESSURE TERMS
    OF THE INTEGRATED FORM OF THE MOMENTUM INTEGRAL EQUATION DIFFERED
    BY NO MORE THAN 19%, AND COMMONLY DIFFERED BY LESS THAN 14%.  THE
    OFF-CENTERLINE VELOCITY PROFILES WERE INDISTINGUISHABLE FROM THOSE
    ON THE CENTERLINE.  THE FLOW FIELD WAS SURVEYED WITH HOT-WIRE ANEMOM-
    ETERS FOR FLUCTUATING COMPONENTS U2, V2, W2 AS WELL AS FOR U2 SPECTRA.
    THE SURFACE SKIN FRICTION TECHNIQUES THAT WERE USED ARE WELL DOCUMENTED,
    WITH GOOD AGREEMENT AMONG RESULTS FROM DIFFERENT METHODS.
    THE PRESSURE COEFFICIENT, CP = 2 * (P(X) - P(0)) / (RO * U(0) ** 2)
    WAS OBTAINED FROM STATIC TAPS ON THE TUNNEL CENTERLINE DURING A PERIOD
    OF A FEW HOURS; DCP/DX WAS OBTAINED FROM A SPECIAL PRESSURE-GRADIENT
    PROBE DURING A PERIOD OF A FEW DAYS.  FLUCTUATIONS IN P AND RO WERE
    SMALL; RO WAS CORRECTED FOR PRESSURE AND TEMPERATURE CHANGES, BUT
    WAS UNCORRECTED FOR HUMIDITY; D2CP/DX2 WAS OBTAINED BY DIFFER-
    ENTIATION OF A SMOOTHED DCP/DX CURVE.

7.  INSTRUMENTATION:    THE MEAN=VELOCITY PROFILES WERE MEASURED
    WITH A FLATTENED TOTAL-HEAD TUBE AND A SEPARATE STATIC TUBE.
    VIRTUALLY IDENTICAL RESULTS WERE OBTAINED WITH A ROUND TOTAL-HEAD
    TUBE.  A SPECIAL PRESSURE-GRADIENT PROBE (SEE FIGURE 1,
    SAMUEL AND JOUBERT (1974)) WAS USED WHICH CONSISTED OF
    TWO TUBES WITH STATIC PRESSURE HOLES 0.0457 M APART MOUNTED ON A
    LONG STING TO REMOVE THE PROBE FROM THE PRESSURE FIELD OF THE
    TRAVERSING APPARATUS.  A CHATTOCK MANOMETER WAS USED WITH SENS-
    ITIVITY 0.098 N/M**2.  NO SHEAR OR WALL DISPLACEMENT CORRECTIONS
    WERE APPLIED TO THE RESULTS.
        WALL SHEAR STRESS MEASUREMENTS WERE MADE USING PRESTON TUBES WITH
    PATEL'S (1965) CALIBRATION, CLAUSER PLOTS OF THE MEAN-VELOCITY
    PROFILES, AND THE FLOATING ELEMENT OF BROWN AND JOUBERT (1969).
        THE REYNOLDS' STRESSES WERE MEASURED USING CONSTANT-TEMPERATURE
    HOT-WIRE ANEMOMETERS; U2 USING A NORMAL WIRE, V2, W2 AND U1V1 USING A
    SINGLE INCLINED WIRE.  THE WIRES WERE 4 MICROMETERS IN DIAMETER
    AND 0.1E-3 M LONG (WOLLASTON WIRE).  SPECIALLY DESIGNED
    TRAVERSING EQUIPMENT WAS USED FOR ACCURATE PROBE POSITION-
    ING AND RELIABLE REPEATABILITY.  THE DYNAMIC CALIBRATION PROCEDURES
    USED WERE THE SAME AS IN PERRY AND MORRISON (1971) AND MORRISON,
    PERRY AND SAMUEL (1972).  RESULTS WERE THE SAME FOR WIRE RESISTANCE RATIOS
    OF 1.5 AND 2.0.  PROFILES AND CALIBRATION CURVES WERE MATCHED FOR
    TEMPERATURE.

8.  EXPERIMENTAL PARAMETERS:  THE WORKING SECTION WAS 1 M WIDE BY
    0.36 M HIGH.  THE NOMINAL REFERENCE REYNOLDS NUMBER UPSTREAM AT
    THE INLET, WAS 1.7E06 .  THE INVISCID CORE FLOW WAS UNIFORM WITHIN
    +/- 0.7% AND THE FREE-STREAM TURBULENCE INTENSITY WAS 0.3%.  ATMOS-
    PHERIC PRESSURE AND TEMPERATURE WAS 1.08E05 MB AND 20 DEG C, RESPECTIVE-
    LY.

    NORMALIZED DATA ARE CREATED FROM MEASURED DATA AS FOLLOWS:
    XNORM = (X - XMIN)/(XMAX - XMIN)
    ALL NULL DATA ARE WRITTEN AS 2.0.
    THE EQUATION DESCRIBING THE RELATION BETWEEN ACTUAL DATA AND
    THE NORMALIZED DATA ON THE TAPE IS
    X = XMIN + ((XMAX - XMIN) * XNORM)
    WHERE X, XMAX, XMIN AND XNORM ARE REAL.
