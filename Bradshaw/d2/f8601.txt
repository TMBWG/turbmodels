     NORMAL SHOCK-WAVE/TURBULENT BOUNDARY-LAYER INTERACTION
     AT TRANSONIC SPEEDS.

2.   REVISION DATE: NOVEMBER 6, 1981

3.   EVALUATOR:  RUBESIN, M. W., AND HORSTMAN, C. C.;

4.   DATE AND LOCATION OF EXPERIMENT: 1976-1979, NATIONAL AERONAUTICS AND SPACE
     ADMINISTRATION, AMES RESEARCH CENTER, MOFFETT FIELD, CALIFORNIA, USA

5.   ABSTRACT:
          THE EXPERIMENTS WERE CONDUCTED IN A CONSTANT-DIAMETER, AXISYMMETRIC TE
     SECTION LOCATED DOWNSTREAM OF A NOMINAL MACH 1.5 NOZZLE. A NORMAL SHOCK-WAV
     WAS GENERATED BY MEANS OF A HOLLOW, CYLINDRICAL CHOKE OF VARYING CROSS-SECT
     AREA THAT WAS INSERTED INTO THE EXIT OF THE TUBE. THE LOCATION OF THE SHOCK
     WITHIN THE TEST SECTION WAS CONTROLLED BY THE CHOKE POSITION. THE DISTANCE
     BETWEEN THE SHOCK AND THE CHOKE WAS GREATER THAN 1 METER. DATA WERE OBTAINE
     ALONG THE WALLS OF THE TUBE AS WELL AS IN THE MAINSTREAM FLOW.
        FLOW-FIELD SURVEYS OF BOTH THE MEAN AND FLUCTUATING QUANTITIES WERE OBTA
     AT A SINGLE MACH AND REYNOLDS NUMBER AT 8 LOCATIONS THROUGHOUT THE INTERACT
     IN ADDITION, WALL PRESSURE AND SKIN FRICTION MEASUREMENTS WERE MADE FOR MAC
     NUMBERS FROM 1.32 TO 1.48 AND REYNOLDS NUMBERS FROM 8.5*10**6 TO 225*10**6.
     DATA WERE OBTAINED BY OPERATING THE FACILITY IN A BLOWDOWN MODE USING UNHEA
     DRY AIR AT A NOMINAL TEMPERATURE OF 283 K. THE WALL TEMPERATURE WAS ESSENTI
     ADIABATIC. THE TOTAL PRESSURES RANGED FROM 0.34 TO 5.44 ATMOSPHERES.

7.   INSTRUMENTATION:
          VELOCITIES WERE DEDUCED FROM THE PRESSURE SIGNATURES OF CONVENTIONAL P
     PROBES. SKIN FRICTION WAS DETERMINED FROM EITHER A PRESTON TUBE OR BURIED H
     WIRE. THE LATTER TECHNIQUE WAS PARTICULARLY USEFUL IN REGIONS OF LARGE ADVE
     PRESSURE GRADIENT. THE SHEAR STRESS PROFILES WERE DETERMINED USING A SUPPOR
     CROSSED-WIRE PROBE DESCRIBED IN REFERENCE 3
          ALL OF THE ANALOG MEAN-FLOW DATA WERE CONVERTED TO A DIGITAL FORMAT AN
     STORED ON MAGNETIC TAPE FOR SUBSEQUENT COMPUTER ANALYSIS. THE SHEAR PROFILE
     WERE STORED DIRECTLY IN ANALOG FORM AND LATER PROCESSED BY TIME CORRELATING
     EQUIPMENT.

11.  TAPE ORGANIZATION:
          THE TAPE IS A 2400 FOOT, ODD PARITY, PHASE ENCODED,
     UNLABELLED TAPE, WRITTEN AT A DENSITY OF 1600 BITS PER INCH ACCORDING
     TO EBCDIC CODE. THE RECORD FORMAT IS FIXED AND BLOCKED; RECORD
     LENGTH = 80 BYTES; 100 RECORD PER BLOCK; BLOCKSIZE = 8000 BYTES.
          NORMALIZED DATA ARE CREATED FROM MEASURED DATA AS FOLLOWS:
     XNORM = (X - XMIN)/(XMAX - XMIN)
     NORMALIZED VALUES ARE INTEGERIZED BY MULTIPLYING BY 10000 AND
     ROUNDING UP OR DOWN TO THE NEAREST INTEGER.
     IXNORM = XNORM * 10000.
     THUS EACH NORMALIZED DATUM IS WRITTEN ONTO THE TAPE AS AN INTEGER
     VALUE BETWEEN 0 AND 10000.
     ALL NULL DATA ARE WRITTEN AS 20000.
     THE EQUATION DESCRIBING THE RELATION BETWEEN ACTUAL DATA AND
     THE NORMALIZED DATA ON THE TAPE IS
     X = XMIN + (((XMAX - XMIN) * IXNORM)/10000.)
     WHERE X, XMAX AND XMIN ARE REAL AND IXNORM IS AN INTEGER.
