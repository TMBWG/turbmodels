      5.     ABSTRACT OF EXPERIMENT
                MEASUREMENTS WERE MADE IN THE TURBULENT BOUNDARY LAYER
             ON A WALL WHICH HAS MILD CURVATURE. THE BOUNDARY
             LAYER THICKNESS TO RADIUS OF CURVATURE RATIO AT THE
             START OF CURVATURE WAS APPROXIMATELY 0.01 . THE DATA
             INCLUDES PROFILES OF MEAN STREAMWISE VELOCITY AND THEIR
             INTEGRAL PARAMETERS FOR FIVE STREAMWISE STATIONS AND
             PROFILES OF SECOND AND THIRD ORDER FLUCTUATING VELOCITY
             QUANTITIES. FOURTH ORDER QUANTITIES WERE PUBLISHED BUT
             NOT ENTERED ON THIS FILE. THE TUNNEL HEIGHT WAS 0.127 M
             AND THE SPAN WAS 0.762 M.

                THE LOCAL STATIC PRESSURE USED IN THE MEAN VELOCITY
             MEASUREMENT IS DEDUCED FROM THE WALL STATIC PRESSURE
             BY USING THE EULER-N EQUATION ASSUMING POTENTIAL FLOW:
             (PTOT-PSTAT)/(PTOT-PSW)=EXP(-2*Y/R)
             AS DONE BY SO AND MELLOR. THEN: U**2=2*(PTOT-PSTAT)/RHO
             WHERE PTOT IS THE LOCAL TOTAL PRESSURE, PSTAT IS THE LOCAL
             STATIC PRESSURE, PSW IS THE WALL STATIC PRESSURE, Y IS THE
             DISTANCE NORMAL TO THE WALL AND R IS THE RADIUS OF CURVATURE
             OF THE WALL

                THE AUTHORS PRESENT PROFILES UPSTREAM OF CURVATURE.
             THE UPSTREAM PROFILE ON THE WALL WHICH BECOMES THE
             CONCAVE WALL IS LABELED PRE-CONCAVE AND THE PROFILE ON
             THE WALL WHICH BECOMES THE CONVEX WALL IS LABELED PRE-
             CONVEX.  THE PRE-CONVEX DATA ARE FOR MEAN VELOCITY ONLY,
             WHEREAS PRE-CONCAVE DATA INCLUDE MEAN AS WELL AS
             FLUCTUATING QUANTITIES.  THE PRE-CONCAVE FLUCTUATING
             QUANTITY PROFILES ARE REPRESENTATIVE OF THE CONVEX WALL
             AS WELL.
                THE PRESSURE COEFFICIENT DATA AND THE SKIN FRICTION
             COEFFICIENT DATA WERE DIGITIZED FROM GRAPHS USING A
             HOUSTON INSTRUMENTS HI-PAD.

      7.     INSTRUMENTATION
                THE MEAN VELOCITY MEASUREMENTS WERE TAKEN BY USING A
                STAGNATION TUBE TO MEASURE THE TOTAL PRESSURE.  STATIC
                PRESSURE PORTS IN THE TEST WALL WERE USED TO MEASURE
                WALL STATIC PRESSURE.
                FLUCTUATING QUANTITIES WERE MEASURED WITH
                CROSS-WIRE CONSTANT TEMPERATURE HOT-WIRE
                ANEMOMETRY. LINEARIZATION OF THE SIGNALS WAS DONE
                DIGITALLY.

     11.     TAPE ORGANIZATION
                THE TAPE IS A 2400 FOOT, 9-TRACK, ODD PARITY, PHASE ENCODED,
                UNLABELLED TAPE WRITTEN AT A DENSITY OF 1600 BITS PER INCH
                ACCORDING TO EBCDIC CODE. THE RECORD FORMAT IS FIXED AND
                BLOCKED; RECORD LENGTH = 80 BYTES; 100 RECORDS PER BLOCK;
                BLOCKSIZE = 8000 BYTES.
                NORMALIZED DATA ARE CREATED FROM MEASURED DATA AS FOLLOWS:
                XNORM=(X-XMIN)/(XMAX-XMIN)
                NORMALIZED VALUES ARE INTEGERIZED BY MULTIPLYING BY
                10000 AND ROUNDING UP OR DOWN TO NEAREST INTEGER.
                IXNORM=XNORM * 10000.
                THUS EACH NORMALIZED DATUM IS WRITTEN ONTO TAPE AS AN
                INTEGER VALUE BETWEEN 0 AND 10000.
                ALL NULL DATA ARE WRITTEN AS 20000.
                THE EQUATION DESCRIBING THE RELATIONSHIP BETWEEN ACTUAL
                DATA AND NORMALIZED DATA IS:
                X=XMIN + (((XMAX-XMIN) * IXNORM) /10000.)
                WHERE X, XMAX AND XMIN ARE REAL AND IXNORM IS INTEGER.
