    OVER 2-DIMENSIONAL BUMP, M=1.37

2.  REVISION DATE:  OCTOBER 25, 1981

3.  EVALUATORS:  RUBESIN, M. W., AND HORSTMAN, C. C., NASA AMES
    RESEARCH CENTER, MOFFETT FIELD, CA. 94035

4.  DATE AND LOCATION OF EXPERIMENT:  1978-1979, TRANSONIC
    WIND TUNNEL, LABORATOIRE DE MECANIQUE DES FLUIDES DE
    ONERA (OFFICE NATIONAL D'ETUDES ET DE RECHERCHES
    AEROSPATIALES). CHATILLONS-SOUS-BAGNEUX, FRANCE.

5. ABSTRACT: EXPERIMENTAL MEASUREMENTS OF SHOCK WAVE
TURBULENT BOUNDARY LAYER INTERACTION WITH INDUCED
SEPARATION IN A TWO-DIMENSIONAL TRANSONIC CHANNEL. THE
TEST DATA WERE OBTAINED AT A RELATIVELY HIGH VALUE OF THE
FLOW MAXIMUM MACH NUMBER OF 1.42. THE MEASUREMENTS INCLUDE
(A) STAGNATION CONDITIONS (NEARLY CONSTANT DURING ALL THE
TESTS), (B) PRESSURE DISTRIBUTIONS ON THE LOWER AND UPPER
WALLS, (C) AXIAL MACH NUMBER DISTRIBUTIONS IN THE INVISCID
PART OF THE FLOW FIELD, AND (D) MEAN-VELOCITY COMPONENTS
(U,V) AND REYNOLDS TENSOR COMPONENTS (U2,V2,UV) IN THE
DISSIPATIVE LAYER

6. REFERENCES:
            DELERY, J: ANALYSE DU DECOLLEMENT RESULTANT D'UNE
  INTERACTION CHOC-COUCHE LIMITE TURBULENTE EN TRANSONIQUE. LA
  RECHERCHE AEROSPATIALE, NO.6, NOV.-DEC., 1978, PP.305-320.

7. INSTRUMENTATION: THE EXPERIMENT WAS PERFORMED IN A TRANSONIC
WIND TUNNEL CONTINUOUSLY SUPPLIED WITH DESSICATED ATMOSPHERIC
AIR.
(A) THE FLOW FIELD WAS PROBED IN THE PLANE OF SYMMETRY OF THE
CHANNEL (Z=0.060) USING HOLOGRAGHIC INTERFEROMETRY AND MAINLY
LASER DOPPLER VELOCIMETRY. THE VELOCIMETER IS A FOUR-BEAM
TWO-COLOR SYSTEM USING THE DUAL SCATTER MODE OF OPERATION ;
BRAGG CELLS ALLOW THE DETERMINATION OF FLOW DIRECTION. THE
FORWARD SCATTERING MODE IS USED.
(B)FREQUENCY:FREQUENCIES WERE MEASURED USING ELECTRONIC COUNTERS.
THE COUNTERS WERE CONNECTED TO A MINICOMPUTER WHICH PERFORMS THE DATA
PROCESSING:CALCULATION OF VELOCITY HISTOGRAMS, MEAN VALUES, STANDARD
DEVIATIONS, CORRELATIONS.
